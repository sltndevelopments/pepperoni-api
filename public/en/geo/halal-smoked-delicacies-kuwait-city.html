Frequently Asked Questions — Kuwait City

Does your halal certification satisfy Kuwait's import requirements for smoked meat?
Yes. Our Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024 is issued by a recognised Islamic authority and accepted by GCC import regulators. Each shipment is accompanied by veterinary certificates issued by Rosselkhoznadzor and a certificate of origin — the full documentation set required by Kuwait's Ministry of Commerce and Industry for chilled and frozen meat imports.
What MOQ applies for wholesale smoked delicacies shipped to Kuwait City?
Standard export minimum is one refrigerated pallet (~400–500 kg). For HoReCa buyers wanting to trial multiple SKUs — smoked beef, smoked chicken, halal sausages — we offer consolidated slots from 100 kg per line item. Contact us to discuss your specific assortment needs.
How does the 15-day sea transit affect product freshness?
Our modified-atmosphere and vacuum packaging gives a shelf life of up to 45 days at +2°C to +4°C. The 15-day Novorossiysk–Kuwait Port transit leaves ample remaining shelf life for customs clearance, local distribution and display. We ship in dedicated refrigerated containers with temperature logging throughout the voyage.
Can you develop a private label smoked product line for our Kuwait retail brand?
Yes. We offer full Private Label production from 500 kg per SKU, including Arabic-language packaging design, GCC-compliant nutrition panels and barcode support. Pre-production samples are provided before any commercial run, and all halal and food safety certificates are reissued under your brand name.
How do your smoked delicacies perform in Kuwait's premium restaurant and hotel kitchens?
Our products are already used in 5-star hotel kitchens and premium restaurant groups across the GCC. We supply professional formats — whole smoked briskets, portion-ready sliced packs and bulk catering units — and our product development team can work with your executive chef to fine-tune seasoning profiles, smoke intensity and cut specifications for your menu.
