Is your kazylyk halal certificate accepted for import into Egypt?

Our halal certificate is issued by DUM RT (Muslim Spiritual Board of the Republic of Tatarstan), a recognized Islamic authority. We recommend verifying acceptance with your Egyptian import agent, as we also supply the full veterinary and phytosanitary documentation package required by Egyptian customs for processed meat from Russia.

What is the transit time from Kazan to Sharm el-Sheikh?

The standard export route is EXW Kazan → Novorossiysk port → Alexandria (approx. 8 days sea transit) → onward by road to Sharm el-Sheikh. Total lead time from order confirmation to delivery at your warehouse is typically 3–4 weeks including customs clearance. We coordinate with freight forwarders experienced on this corridor.

Can we order kazylyk under our own brand (Private Label)?

Yes. Kazan Delicacies offers full Private Label / SBM manufacturing — custom packaging design, your logo, Arabic-language labeling, and tailored recipe parameters. This option is popular with regional distributors and hotel chains building their own delicatessen lines in the GCC and North Africa.

How should kazylyk be stored and served in a restaurant setting?

Premium казылык is a dry-cured product with extended shelf life at ambient or chilled conditions (consult product spec sheet for exact parameters). In a HoReCa context, it is typically served thinly sliced as part of a charcuterie board, mezze platter, or national cuisine starter — a natural fit for Sharm el-Sheikh's international and Arab guest mix.

Does the product contain any pork derivatives or alcohol?

Categorically no. Kazan Delicacies is a halal-first manufacturer — 100% of our product range is produced without pork, pork derivatives, or alcohol. Kazylyk is made exclusively from halal horse meat. This is verified by halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024 and confirmed in every batch's production documentation.
