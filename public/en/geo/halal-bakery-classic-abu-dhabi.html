Frequently Asked Questions — Abu Dhabi Market

Does your halal certification meet Abu Dhabi's import requirements?

Yes. Our Halal certificate is issued by DUM RT (Spiritual Board of Muslims of the Republic of Tatarstan), number #614A/2024. It aligns with ESMA GSO 2055-1 standards applied to imported halal food products in the UAE. We provide the full certificate in English with every export shipment.

What is the minimum order quantity for wholesale supply to Abu Dhabi?

Standard export MOQ is 500 kg per SKU. For initial trial shipments via air freight, we can discuss smaller quantities — contact our export manager to agree on the specifics for your operation.

How long does delivery from Kazan to Abu Dhabi take?

Air freight from Kazan to Dubai takes approximately 5 hours of flight time, with total logistics of 3–5 business days. Sea freight via Novorossiysk to Jebel Ali takes around 14 days and is the preferred route for regular pallet-volume orders.

Is Private Label available for Abu Dhabi restaurant chains and distributors?

Yes. We offer complete Private Label production including Arabic-language packaging, custom portion formats, and branded SKU development. This is particularly relevant for Abu Dhabi's fast-casual chains, hotel F&B groups, and cloud kitchen operators who want proprietary pastry lines.

What storage and handling conditions are required for your frozen pastries?

Products must be stored at −18°C with a shelf life of 12 months. Packaging is gastronorm-compatible for professional kitchen environments. Items can be cooked directly from frozen — oven, fryer, or combi-steamer — without thawing, which suits high-throughput Abu Dhabi restaurant kitchens.
