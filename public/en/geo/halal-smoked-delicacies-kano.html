Pepperoni Tatar: A Kazan Story Built on Clean Meat

Pepperoni Tatar was founded in Kazan — the capital of Tatarstan, Russia's largest Muslim-majority republic — with one clear principle: halal must mean genuinely halal, not just a stamp. From day one, the brand chose natural smoking over chemical shortcuts, banned sodium nitrite from every recipe, and built its production around international food safety standards. Today, Pepperoni Tatar holds Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024, HACCP, ISO 22000:2018, and full TR CU 021/2011 compliance — and exports to markets where halal integrity is non-negotiable. Kano is exactly that kind of market.

Frequently Asked Questions — Kano & Nigeria

Questions we hear most from Nigerian importers and HoReCa buyers.

DUM RT (Spiritual Board of Muslims of Tatarstan) is a well-established Islamic authority with over 200 years of history. For import into Nigeria, we provide the original halal certificate, a notarised English translation, and veterinary health certificates — the full package required for NAFDAC registration and Kano State customs clearance. Our export managers have experience preparing documentation for African markets.

Products are dispatched from our Kazan facility in refrigerated containers to a Russian port (St. Petersburg or Novorossiysk), then shipped by sea to Lagos — 30 to 40 days total. From Apapa Port in Lagos, goods travel north to Kano by road freight, typically an additional 2–3 days. We work with established freight forwarders and can recommend trusted Nigerian customs brokers.

Kano's upscale hotels — particularly those serving international business travellers and Nigerian corporate clients — have strong demand for premium smoked beef and smoked chicken for breakfast buffets and à la carte menus. Our halal smoked sausages in 500 g vacuum packs are popular with fast-food operators. Chefs consistently highlight the clean flavour profile that comes from natural wood smoking without chemical additives.

Our smoked delicacies require chilled storage at 0–6 °C with a shelf life of up to 45 days from production date. For frozen storage at −18 °C, shelf life extends to 6 months. We recommend Nigerian importers work with cold-chain logistics providers in Lagos and Kano — we can share a list of partners upon request. All products are labelled with full storage instructions in English.
