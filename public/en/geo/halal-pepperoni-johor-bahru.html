FAQ — Johor Bahru Buyers

Is your halal certificate accepted for import into Malaysia?

Our DUM RT Halal certificate #614A/2024 is issued by the Muslim Spiritual Board of the Republic of Tatarstan, a recognised halal authority in the Russian Federation. We provide full documentation to support your local importer's JAKIM review process, including production audit reports and ingredient traceability records.

What minimum quantities can I order for a trial shipment to JB?

Via air freight, we accept trial orders from 50 kg — enough to run a two-week kitchen test across multiple pizza SKUs. Sea freight consolidation starts at approximately 500 kg. We recommend an air trial first, then switching to sea for regular replenishment.

Can you adjust the spice profile for Malaysian taste preferences?

Yes. Malaysian pizza consumers often prefer a milder heat level compared to Middle Eastern markets. Through our Private Label programme, we can calibrate paprika ratio, chilli intensity and smoke note to match your target profile. Recipe development samples are available before full production commitment.

How is the pepperoni packaged for professional kitchen use?

Standard HoReCa packs are 2 kg and 5 kg vacuum-sealed, with interleaved parchment sheets for easy single-slice dispensing on a busy topping line. Slice diameter is held to ±1 mm tolerance. Shelf life is 90 days frozen, 21 days chilled after opening.

Do you work with Malaysian distributors or only direct with restaurants?

We work with both. For Johor Bahru, we are actively seeking a local halal food distributor to serve the JB HoReCa market. Direct restaurant chains with sufficient volume (500 kg/month+) can also order directly. Contact us to discuss the model that fits your structure.
