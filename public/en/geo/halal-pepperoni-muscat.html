FAQ — Halal Pepperoni for Muscat & Oman

Questions we receive most often from Omani distributors and HoReCa buyers.

Is your halal certificate accepted by Omani authorities and import regulators?

Our halal certificate is issued by DUM RT — the Muslim Spiritual Board of the Republic of Tatarstan, an internationally recognised Islamic body. We recommend confirming acceptance with your Omani customs broker; we provide all certificate copies in English and can supply Arabic translations on request.

How long does delivery from Kazan to Muscat take, and what are the logistics options?

Sea freight via Novorossiysk to Muscat takes approximately 18 days in refrigerated containers. We ship in export-grade vacuum packs rated for Gulf ambient temperatures during port handling. Air freight is available for sample orders or urgent top-up shipments.

What pepperoni diameters and slice formats work best for Muscat's pizza restaurants?

We produce diameters from 40 mm to 75 mm. For Muscat's casual dining and fast-food pizza formats, the 50–55 mm sliced range is most popular. Hotel F&B and premium casual dining venues often prefer the 70–75 mm format for visual impact. We can produce custom slice thickness per your chef's specification.

Can we order pepperoni under our own brand for the Omani market?

Yes — Private Label (SBM) manufacturing is a core service. We handle recipe customisation, Arabic-language labelling, export packaging design and branded vacuum packs. This is the same service we provide to major Russian retail and HoReCa clients. Contact us for MOQ details and a Private Label proposal.

What are the minimum order quantities for export to Oman?

MOQ for export orders is discussed individually based on product mix and logistics format. We can combine multiple SKUs (pepperoni, halal sausages, burger patties) into one container to reach cost-effective freight thresholds. Contact our export team for a tailored commercial offer.
