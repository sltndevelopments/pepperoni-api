Frequently Asked Questions — Ankara

Do you supply halal pastries directly to restaurants and cafés in Ankara?

Yes. We work with Ankara HoReCa operators — from independent restaurants to hotel banquet kitchens and fast-food chains. Minimum order starts from one pallet. We provide all documentation needed for Turkish customs and halal compliance audits.

How does delivery from Kazan to Ankara work, and how long does it take?

We ship frozen goods via the Novorossiysk–Samsun ferry route in refrigerated containers, then continue by reefer truck to Ankara. Total transit is 2–4 days depending on the ferry schedule. The cold chain is maintained at −18 °C throughout the journey.

Which halal authority issued your certificate, and is it accepted in Turkey?

Our halal certificate is issued by the Spiritual Board of Muslims of the Republic of Tatarstan (DUM RT), certificate #614A/2024. DUM RT is a well-established Islamic authority whose documents are recognized by Turkish halal certification bodies and accepted by importers across the Middle East.

Can you develop a Private Label pastry line for our Turkish distribution brand?

Absolutely. We offer full Private Label service: your brand name, bilingual Turkish/Russian packaging, custom portion weights and carton sizes. This is a popular option for Ankara-based distributors entering the frozen HoReCa segment. Minimum run and timelines are discussed individually.

Why is the absence of sodium nitrite important for the Ankara food market?

Ankara's premium restaurant segment and health-focused fast-casual brands increasingly demand clean-label ingredients — a trend mirroring Istanbul and European markets. Our pastries contain zero sodium nitrite, no artificial colors, and no GMO components, making them a strong fit for menus that highlight natural, wholesome ingredients.
