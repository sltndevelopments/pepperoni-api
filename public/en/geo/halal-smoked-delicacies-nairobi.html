Frequently Asked Questions — Nairobi & Kenya

Do your smoked products meet Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS) import requirements?

Yes. Our products carry ISO 22000:2018, HACCP and TR CU 021/2011 documentation, which aligns with KEBS food safety import requirements. We also provide full Russian veterinary certificates and halal certification DUM RT #614A/2024 accepted across East Africa.

How is the product delivered to Nairobi?

We ship via sea freight to the Port of Mombasa — transit time is 28–35 days from Kazan. From Mombasa, refrigerated road transport reaches Nairobi warehouses within 1–2 days. We coordinate with your local logistics partner or can recommend trusted freight forwarders experienced on this corridor.

Is the halal certificate recognised by East African halal bodies?

Our halal certificate is issued by DUM RT (Spiritual Board of Muslims of Tatarstan), number #614A/2024. It is internationally recognised and has been accepted by importers, distributors and halal-conscious retail chains across Kenya, Tanzania and Uganda without issues.

What packaging formats are available for Nairobi restaurants and hotel kitchens?

We offer vacuum-sealed professional HoReCa packs (0.5–2 kg), modified atmosphere packaging for extended shelf life, and gift-set retail formats suitable for premium supermarkets. Private Label is available for chains and distributors who want their own brand identity on the product.

What is the minimum order quantity for wholesale export to Kenya?

The minimum export order is one refrigerated 20-foot container, approximately 8–10 tonnes of mixed product. We can combine копчёная говядина оптом, smoked chicken and халяль копчёная колбаса оптом in one shipment to optimise your assortment and reduce per-unit logistics cost.
