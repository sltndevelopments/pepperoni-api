Does your halal certificate satisfy Indonesian BPJPH requirements?

Our DUM RT certificate #614A/2024 is issued by an internationally recognized Muslim authority. While final BPJPH registration is the importer's responsibility under Indonesian law, we provide the full halal audit trail — including production records and ingredient traceability — that supports your local registration application. We have experience supporting importers in GCC markets with similar regulatory requirements.

What is the minimum order for wholesale kazylyk export to Jakarta?

The minimum export order is one consolidated pallet (approximately 200–400 kg depending on packaging format). For trial orders, we can arrange LCL (less-than-container-load) sea freight. Contact us for a specific quote based on your required volume and delivery address.

How does kazylyk fit into a Jakarta restaurant or specialty food store menu?

Kazylyk's dense, savory, slightly smoky profile makes it an ideal addition to charcuterie boards, mezze platters, and fusion appetizers — a format that is growing fast in Jakarta's mid-to-premium dining segment. For specialty food retail (such as premium supermarkets and online gourmet stores), the craft gift packaging positions kazylyk as a high-margin imported delicacy with a compelling origin story.

What are the shelf life and cold chain requirements for the Indonesian climate?

Export vacuum-sealed kazylyk has a shelf life of up to 180 days at +2°C to +6°C. For Indonesia's tropical climate, we recommend continuous cold-chain storage throughout distribution. We provide full cold-chain documentation and can advise on packaging specifications suited to local warehousing conditions.
