FAQ — Tatar Pastries Wholesale, Dhaka

Is your Tatar pastry halal-certified for the Bangladesh market?

Yes. All products carry Halal certificate #614A/2024 issued by DUM RT — the Muslim Spiritual Board of the Republic of Tatarstan. No pork, no alcohol, 100% halal beef and poultry ingredients. We can provide the original certificate and its English translation for your importer's customs filing.

How long does sea freight from Kazan to Dhaka take?

Standard sea freight EXW Kazan to Chittagong port takes 25–35 days depending on routing and consolidation. With a 180-day frozen shelf life, products arrive with ample time for distribution and kitchen use. We prepare all export documentation on our side — you handle import clearance in Bangladesh.

What is the minimum order quantity for export to Bangladesh?

MOQ for export is typically one consolidated pallet per SKU. For first-time Dhaka buyers we can discuss LCL (less-than-container-load) options to reduce initial investment. Contact our export team to build a trial order structure that fits your storage capacity.

Can Dhaka restaurants or distributors order private-label Tatar pastries?

Absolutely. We offer full private-label (SBM) production: custom recipe adaptation, packaging design, and labelling in English or Bengali. This is ideal for Dhaka restaurant groups, ghost kitchens, or food importers who want to launch a unique ethnic cuisine line under their own brand.

What storage and kitchen conditions are required for echpochmak and peremyach?

Products are supplied frozen at −18 °C. Professional HoReCa packaging is designed for commercial freezer and blast-chiller storage. Echpochmak and peremyach can be reheated directly in a convection oven — no deep-frying required for most SKUs. We provide a full product sheet with reheating protocols for your kitchen team.
