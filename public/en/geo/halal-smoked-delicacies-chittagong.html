Frequently Asked Questions — Chittagong Buyers

Is your halal certification accepted by Bangladeshi authorities and retail chains?
Our Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024 is issued by the Spiritual Board of Muslims of the Republic of Tatarstan — a recognised international halal body. Bangladeshi importers, hotel procurement departments and retail buyers in Chittagong and Dhaka routinely accept this certification. We provide the original certificate with each shipment.
How does sea freight cold-chain work from Kazan to Chittagong port?
Products leave our facility in Kazan in refrigerated transport, transferred to a refrigerated sea container at the Russian port. Transit to Chittagong takes 25–35 days at a controlled temperature of 0–4°C for chilled products. We work with established freight forwarders experienced in food export to South Asia.
We run a hotel kitchen in Chittagong — what formats do you offer for professional use?
We produce specifically for HoReCa: whole smoked beef and chicken pieces in vacuum packs (1–3 kg), pre-sliced portions in MAP trays for banquet service, and bulk catering formats for high-volume kitchens. Our team can advise on the right SKU mix for your menu and storage conditions.
Why does it matter that your products contain no sodium nitrite?
Sodium nitrite (E250) is a synthetic preservative widely used in smoked meats — but increasingly rejected by health-aware consumers, premium hotel brands and clean-label retail buyers across Bangladesh. Our natural wood-smoking process achieves preservation without E250, giving your brand a genuine clean-label story that competitors using conventional curing cannot match.
Can we launch a Private Label smoked delicacy brand for the Bangladeshi market?
Absolutely. We offer full Private Label from 1,000 kg — your brand name, Bangla-language labelling, your packaging design. We handle all production, halal certification and export documentation. This is an efficient route to market for Chittagong-based importers who want a differentiated premium product without building their own production infrastructure.
