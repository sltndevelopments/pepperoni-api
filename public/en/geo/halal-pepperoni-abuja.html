FAQ — Abuja & Nigeria

Specific questions from Nigerian HoReCa buyers and distributors

Is your pepperoni certified halal for the Nigerian market?

Yes. Our pepperoni carries Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024 issued by the Muslim Spiritual Board of the Republic of Tatarstan. The product contains zero pork, zero alcohol, and zero hidden haram ingredients — fully compliant with requirements of Nigeria's Muslim consumer base. The certificate is provided in English with every export shipment.

How is pepperoni delivered from Kazan to Abuja?

We ship EXW Kazan. Sea freight to Lagos port takes approximately 30–40 days. From Lagos the cargo is cleared and trucked to Abuja. We provide full export documentation including Russian veterinary certificates and halal certificates for Nigerian customs clearance. Our logistics partners have active experience on this corridor.

What minimum order quantity (MOQ) is required for export to Nigeria?

MOQ for export orders is typically one full pallet (approx. 400–500 kg depending on SKU). For first trial orders we can discuss consolidated shipment options with other African-bound cargo. Contact us at info@kazandelikates.tatar for a tailored commercial offer in USD or EUR.

Can we order pepperoni under our own Nigerian brand (Private Label)?

Absolutely. Private Label is one of our core services. We adapt recipe, casing diameter (40–75 mm), slice thickness, packaging design and label language for your Nigerian brand. Minimum run from one production batch. Full English-language label with halal mark and all required import declarations is standard.

What storage conditions does the pepperoni require in an Abuja kitchen?

Sliced pepperoni in vacuum MAP packaging: 0–6 °C, shelf life 45–60 days. Whole sticks: 0–6 °C, shelf life up to 90 days. We supply professional HoReCa packs (1–2 kg vacuum) optimised for kitchen portioning in high-volume Abuja restaurants. For operations without reliable cold chain, whole sticks offer greater flexibility.
