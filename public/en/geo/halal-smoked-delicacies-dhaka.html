Frequently Asked Questions — Dhaka Buyers

Does your halal certification meet Bangladesh's import requirements?

Yes. Our halal certificate is issued by DUM RT (Spiritual Board of Muslims of Tatarstan), #614A/2024. It is internationally recognised and accepted by Bangladeshi importers and BSTI-registered distributors. We provide the original plus a notarised English translation with every shipment.

How is the product delivered to Dhaka from Kazan?

We ship via refrigerated sea freight to Chittagong port — the main entry point for all chilled and frozen food imports into Bangladesh. Transit time is 25–35 days. From Chittagong, goods typically reach Dhaka warehouses within 24–48 hours. We coordinate with your nominated freight forwarder or can recommend established partners.

Why are your smoked products free of sodium nitrite when most competitors use it?

Nitrite-free production requires precise temperature management and shorter shelf windows — it costs more to do correctly. We made that investment because our core clients in HoReCa — hotel chefs, premium restaurant groups — specifically require clean-label proteins. For Dhaka's growing fine-dining segment, this is increasingly a purchasing criterion, not just a preference.

What is the minimum order quantity for Dhaka HoReCa buyers?

Minimum order for international wholesale is 500 kg per SKU. For mixed containers combining smoked beef, chicken and sausage lines, we discuss MOQ individually based on your storage capacity and sales cycle. Private Label orders start from 1,000 kg per SKU.

Can you develop custom packaging and labelling for the Bangladeshi retail market?

Absolutely. Our Private Label service covers full packaging design — your brand name, English and Bengali text, barcodes compliant with Bangladeshi retail standards, and net weights adapted to local consumer formats. We have done this for distributors in several Muslim-majority markets and the process typically takes 3–4 weeks from brief to approved artwork.
