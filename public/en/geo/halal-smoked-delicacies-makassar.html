Frequently Asked Questions — Makassar & Indonesia

Is your halal certification recognized under Indonesia's BPJPH framework?

Our Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024 is issued by a recognized Islamic authority. We provide the full documentation package — including certificate translations and product composition declarations — to support your BPJPH registration and Indonesian customs clearance. Our export team has experience guiding partners through this process.

What is the minimum wholesale order for delivery to Makassar?

The minimum order for a sea freight shipment is one pallet, approximately 500 kg. We regularly consolidate orders for Southeast Asian buyers, which helps Makassar-based distributors manage freight costs efficiently without committing to full container loads from the start.

How are products packaged for long-distance sea freight to Indonesia?

All smoked delicacies are vacuum-sealed or MAP-packed and dispatched in insulated export cartons. Products are frozen prior to shipment to maintain quality over the 35–45 day transit to Jakarta or Surabaya. Cold chain integrity documentation is included with every shipment.

Can Makassar restaurants and hotel kitchens order under a private label?

Absolutely. Private Label is available for HoReCa groups, hotel chains and retail distributors operating in Makassar and across South Sulawesi. We adapt packaging format, portion size and labeling to your specification. Discuss your requirements directly with our export team.

Why does the absence of sodium nitrite matter for the Indonesian HoReCa market?

Makassar's premium restaurant segment and health-conscious café culture are increasingly demanding clean-label ingredients. Sodium nitrite-free products allow chefs to communicate transparent sourcing to guests and align with the stricter halal and wellness positioning that drives purchasing decisions in Indonesia's fast-growing F&B sector.
