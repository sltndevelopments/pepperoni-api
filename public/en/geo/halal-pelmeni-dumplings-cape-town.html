Frequently Asked Questions — Cape Town Buyers

Is your halal certification recognised in South Africa by SANHA or MJC?

Our certification is issued by DUM RT, a credentialed Islamic authority. South African importers operating under SANHA or MJC oversight can review our full documentation package — certificate, audit reports and production line scope — before placing an order. We regularly work with buyers who require multi-authority verification.

How does frozen cargo reach Cape Town and what is the lead time?

We ship in refrigerated (reefer) containers, typically routing through Durban port — the closest major South African container terminal — with an estimated transit of 30–40 days. From Durban, cargo moves by refrigerated road freight to Cape Town. We manage all export documentation including veterinary certificates and customs declarations from our side.

What minimum order quantity applies for Cape Town restaurant or retail buyers?

For export orders, the minimum is one full pallet (approximately 500 kg). Full reefer container orders (typically 20–22 tonnes) receive preferential per-kg pricing. We are open to discussing consolidated shipments with Cape Town-based distributors who aggregate orders from multiple clients.

Can you produce private label pelmeni for our Cape Town retail or food-service brand?

Yes — Private Label is a core part of our offer. We handle everything from packaging artwork integration to certification transfer under your brand name. This is particularly relevant for Western Cape distributors looking to launch a branded halal frozen range without building their own production capacity.

How do chefs and kitchen teams typically use your pelmeni in a Cape Town restaurant context?

Our pelmeni cook from frozen in 7–9 minutes in boiling water — no thawing required. They work equally well pan-fried or in broth-based dishes, making them versatile for fusion menus common in Cape Town's diverse dining scene. We can provide technical sheets and chef guidance notes with every commercial order.
