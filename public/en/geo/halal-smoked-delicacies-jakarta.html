Frequently Asked Questions — Jakarta Importers

Is your halal certification recognised for import into Indonesia under BPJPH rules?

Our Halal certificate (DUM RT No. 614A/2024) is issued by an accredited Islamic authority and is submitted as part of the BPJPH-required halal documentation package. We work closely with Indonesian importers to prepare the full compliance dossier, including translated certificates and product composition declarations.

What is the realistic delivery lead time to Jakarta's Tanjung Priok port?

Sea freight from our production facility to Jakarta typically takes 35–45 days, including loading, transit and port handling. We recommend planning orders 6–8 weeks ahead to ensure uninterrupted stock for your HoReCa or retail operations.

Do your smoked products contain sodium nitrite or other preservatives?

No. Our entire range of smoked halal delicacies is produced without sodium nitrite (E250). We rely on natural smoking and clean-label formulations — no GMO ingredients, no artificial colours, no hidden chemical preservatives. This is a key differentiator for health-conscious Jakarta consumers.

Can you produce under our brand (Private Label) for the Indonesian retail market?

Absolutely. Private Label production is available from one container. We handle formulation, certified production, packaging design coordination and labelling in compliance with Indonesian food labelling requirements. This is a popular option for Jakarta distributors building proprietary product lines.

Which smoked products are most in demand for Jakarta's restaurant and hotel kitchens?

Based on our HoReCa export experience, the highest demand is for naturally smoked beef (brisket and round cuts), smoked halal chicken breast, and smoked sausages in bulk catering formats. We can advise on SKU selection and portion sizing specifically for Jakarta's food service standards.
