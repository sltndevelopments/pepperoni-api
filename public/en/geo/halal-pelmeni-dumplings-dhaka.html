Frequently Asked Questions — Dhaka & Bangladesh

Answers from our export team based on real enquiries from Bangladeshi buyers.

Is your Halal certificate recognised by Bangladeshi authorities and importers?

Our certificate DUM RT #614A/2024 is issued by the Spiritual Board of Muslims of the Republic of Tatarstan, a recognised Islamic authority. We provide full Arabic and English translations for Bangladeshi importer review and BSTI submission. To date, our documentation has cleared customs in multiple Muslim-majority markets without issue.

How does sea freight from Kazan to Dhaka work in practice?

Goods leave our Kazan facility in a reefer container, transit to a Russian port, and arrive at Chittagong in 25–35 days. Onward road delivery from Chittagong to Dhaka takes approximately 4–5 hours. We prepare a full document set: veterinary certificate, halal certificate, certificate of origin, packing list, and commercial invoice — ready for your customs broker.

What pack sizes work best for Dhaka restaurant kitchens?

Most Dhaka HoReCa clients prefer 5 kg catering packs — easy to portion, fits standard commercial freezers, and reduces waste in kitchens that run multiple menu items. For high-volume canteens and institutional catering (e.g., corporate parks in Uttara or Tejgaon), 10 kg bulk packs offer better per-kg economics.

Can Bangladeshi distributors order a mixed container with other product lines?

Yes. We support mixed reefer container shipments combining pelmeni with other frozen meat products from our catalogue. This allows new partners to reach the 20 MT container MOQ without committing to a single SKU. Contact our export manager to build a product mix that fits your distribution network.

Why does your pelmeni contain no sodium nitrite — and why does it matter for the Bangladeshi market?

Sodium nitrite (E250) is a preservative widely used in processed meat but increasingly scrutinised by health-conscious consumers and regulatory bodies across South Asia. Our production technology achieves preservation through rapid blast-freezing at −35 °C rather than chemical additives. This clean-label positioning is a strong retail differentiator in Dhaka's growing premium grocery segment.
