Frequently Asked Questions — Muscat & Oman

Is your halal certificate recognized for import into Oman?

Our halal certification is issued by DUM RT, certificate #614A/2024 — a recognized Islamic authority in Russia. For Omani customs, we recommend coordinating with your licensed importer to confirm acceptance with MOCI as part of standard clearance; we provide all supporting halal documentation in English and Arabic.

How long does sea freight from Russia to Muscat take, and what are the logistics?

Sea freight via Novorossiysk to Port Sultan Qaboos takes approximately 18 days in a refrigerated container. We supply EXW Kazan; your freight forwarder or our logistics partner manages the full chain. We prepare all veterinary export certificates and halal documentation in-house.

Do your pizza toppings perform in deck ovens used across Muscat's pizzerias?

Yes — this was a core design requirement. Our toppings are formulated for thermal stability at 280–320 °C. Calibrated slicing (±0.3 mm tolerance) ensures uniform heat distribution; slices hold moisture, do not curl, and maintain consistent colour. We can provide test samples for your kitchen team before placing a commercial order.

What is the minimum order for export to Oman, and in which currency do you invoice?

The standard minimum export order is one refrigerated FCL (20-foot container), with the ability to mix SKUs. We invoice in USD, AED, or OMR depending on your preference. Payment terms are discussed individually for regular distributors.

Can you produce private-label halal pizza toppings for the Omani market under our brand?

Yes. We offer full SBM/private-label manufacturing — custom recipe, slice thickness, packaging design, and Arabic-language labelling. Several GCC partners already carry our production under their own brands. Send your technical specification to info@kazandelikates.tatar and we will respond within 24 hours with a feasibility confirmation.
