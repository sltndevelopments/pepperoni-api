Frequently Asked Questions — Khartoum & Sudan

Is the Halal certificate recognised by Sudanese food import authorities?

Yes. The DUM RT #614A/2024 certificate is issued by a recognised Islamic authority and is accepted by Sudanese importers and the relevant regulatory bodies. We can provide a translated copy of the certificate upon request to support your import registration process.

How long does the full delivery cycle to Khartoum take?

Sea freight from our facility in Kazan to Port Sudan takes 20–28 days. Add 3–5 days for production lead time and 2–4 days for inland transport from Port Sudan to Khartoum. Total cycle from order confirmation: approximately 30–38 days for the first shipment, faster for repeat orders with pre-production.

What minimum order quantity makes sense for a Khartoum distributor?

We recommend starting with a mixed pallet order of at least 200 kg per SKU to cover sea freight economics. For full container loads (FCL), the economics improve significantly. Many Khartoum distributors begin with an LCL trial order across 3–4 product lines to test market response before committing to FCL volumes.

Can Khartoum hotel chains and restaurant groups work with us directly without a local distributor?

Yes. We work with HoReCa buyers directly, provided they have import capability or work with a licensed Sudanese food importer. We can recommend logistics partners with experience in the Khartoum market. Our HoReCa packaging formats (200 g, 500 g, 1 kg vacuum packs) are designed for professional kitchen use.

Why does "no sodium nitrite" matter specifically for the Sudanese market?

Health-conscious consumption is growing rapidly among Khartoum's urban middle class and the expatriate community. Upmarket restaurants, hotel restaurants and premium retail formats increasingly require clean-label products they can market honestly to guests. Our nitrite-free smoked meats give Khartoum buyers a genuine product story — not just a commodity.
