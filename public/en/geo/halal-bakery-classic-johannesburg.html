Frequently Asked Questions — Johannesburg & South Africa

Is your halal certification recognised by South African halal bodies such as SANHA?

Our products carry Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024, issued by a recognised Islamic authority. We provide full documentation to support your SANHA or MJC verification process, and our export team has experience navigating South African halal import requirements.

How does sea freight to Johannesburg work and how long does it take?

Frozen goods travel in reefer containers from Russian ports to Durban — South Africa's main import gateway — in 30–40 days. Road transport from Durban to Johannesburg adds approximately 1–2 days. We coordinate with established freight forwarders experienced in cold-chain logistics on this corridor.

What is the minimum order for halal baked goods wholesale?

The minimum order is one full pallet (approximately 500 kg). Mixed pallets combining pies, samsa and chebureki are accepted — ideal for distributors or large restaurant groups in Johannesburg testing multiple SKUs before committing to a full container.

Can you produce under our brand for our Johannesburg café or restaurant chain?

Yes. Private Label production is fully available. We develop packaging that meets South African food labelling regulations, including bilingual English/Afrikaans requirements, allergen declarations and all mandatory halal markings. Minimum Private Label volumes start from one pallet.

How are the products packaged for professional kitchen use?

We offer catering-format cartons (30–50 pieces), IQF bulk bags and vacuum-sealed trays. All formats are designed for high-volume kitchen environments. Cooking instructions are provided in English, and our technical team can advise on convection oven or deep-fry settings for each product.
