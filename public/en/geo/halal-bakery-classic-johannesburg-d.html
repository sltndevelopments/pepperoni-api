❓ FAQ — halal classic bakery for Johannesburg

1. How does your halal certification compare to SANHA requirements?

Our DUM RT #614A/2024 is accepted by SANHA as equivalent – we follow the same Zabiha standards, with separate production lines and no alcohol-based additives. We provide a halal letter for each container.

2. What is the shelf life of frozen pirozhki and samsa?

Frozen classic pastries: 12 months at -18°C. Ready-to-eat (baked): 5 days chilled. We recommend blast freezing for Johannesburg distribution.

3. Do you offer private label for South African brands?

Yes. We pack under your brand for retail or HoReCa. Minimum order: 1 pallet (approx. 800 kg). Custom packaging with halal logo, nutritional info, and barcode.

4. What is the typical shipping time to Johannesburg?

Sea freight from Russia to Durban port: 30–40 days. Then ~6h truck to Johannesburg. We handle all veterinary and halal documentation. FOB / CIF terms.

5. Can you supply samples to HoReCa buyers in Johannesburg?

Absolutely. We send frozen samples via air freight (5–7 days). You test pirozhki, chebureki, samsa – then place bulk order. We support with spec sheets and baking instructions.
