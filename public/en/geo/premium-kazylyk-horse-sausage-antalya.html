Yes. Our kazylyk carries Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024, issued by the Muslim Spiritual Board of the Republic of Tatarstan. The product is 100% horse meat — no pork derivatives, no alcohol in any processing stage. The certificate is accepted by GCC and CIS halal authorities, making it relevant for Antalya's Arab Gulf hotel guests as well as the local Muslim population.
How is kazylyk delivered to Antalya, and how long does it take?
We ship EXW Kazan. The primary route uses the Novorossiysk–Samsun ferry (2–4 days at sea), then refrigerated road freight to Antalya (6–8 hours). We partner with freight forwarders experienced in Russian–Turkish food logistics and cold-chain requirements. Full transit documentation is prepared for Turkish customs clearance.
What shelf life and storage does premium kazylyk require in a restaurant kitchen?
Naturally cured kazylyk has a shelf life of up to 90 days at +2°C to +6°C in vacuum packaging. The clean-label formulation — no sodium nitrite, no artificial preservatives — requires strict cold-chain maintenance. We provide full cold-chain documentation and recommend storage protocols to your receiving team.

Yes. We offer full Private Label (SBM) manufacturing: custom spice profile, portion weight, casing type, and branded vacuum packaging. Most kazylyk reaching Turkey comes from small Central Asian workshops without industrial food safety certification.
