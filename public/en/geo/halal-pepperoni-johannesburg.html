FAQ — Halal Pepperoni Import to Johannesburg

Answers to the questions South African buyers ask most often.

Is your halal certificate accepted for import into South Africa under SANHA requirements?
Our DUM RT (Muslim Spiritual Board of Tatarstan) certificate #614A/2024 covers the full production chain. We provide a complete documentation package — ingredient declarations, slaughter certificates, and lot-level traceability — to support your SANHA-guided import compliance review. We recommend involving your local halal certifier early in the process; we have experience preparing the required file.
What is the minimum order quantity for a first wholesale shipment to Johannesburg?
Standard export MOQ is one full pallet (approximately 500–600 kg net). For first-time South African buyers, we offer a structured trial order format — typically 1–2 pallets — so your kitchen team can validate performance before committing to a full container. Contact us to structure the right entry-level order.
How does the cold chain work from Kazan to Johannesburg restaurants?
Product ships in refrigerated containers (0–4 °C) from Kazan to Durban port — approximately 30–40 days at sea. From Durban to Johannesburg is 1–2 days by refrigerated truck. Our vacuum-sealed export packaging maintains product integrity throughout, and the 90-day shelf life at 0–4 °C provides comfortable buffer for customs clearance and last-mile distribution.
Can you produce pepperoni under our South African brand (Private Label)?
Absolutely. We offer full Private Label manufacturing with custom recipe development, casing diameter selection (40–75 mm), slice thickness, English-language packaging design, and halal logo placement. Minimum run starts at 300 kg per SKU. This is particularly relevant for Johannesburg distributors looking to launch their own halal deli brand in retail or food service.
Does your pepperoni contain pork, nitrites, or artificial additives?
No pork — ever. Our facility is a 100% halal-only production environment; pork is categorically absent. The pepperoni is also free of sodium nitrite and artificial colors, meeting the clean-label standards that premium Johannesburg HoReCa operators and halal-conscious retail buyers increasingly specify in their supplier requirements.
