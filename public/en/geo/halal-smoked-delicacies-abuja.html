Frequently Asked Questions — Abuja Market

Does the product meet halal requirements for the Nigerian Muslim market?

Yes. All products carry Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024 from the Muslim Religious Board of Tatarstan — a recognised Islamic authority. There are no pork derivatives, no sodium nitrite, and the full ingredient declaration is available for importer review. This is particularly important for distribution into Abuja's Muslim-majority consumer base and onward supply to northern Nigerian states.

How is delivery to Abuja organised from Kazan?

We ship via refrigerated sea freight container to Lagos (Apapa or Tin Can Island port), with a transit time of 30–40 days. From Lagos, goods are transported by refrigerated road truck to Abuja — a well-established corridor used by Nigerian FMCG importers. We coordinate all export logistics and provide full documentation for Nigerian customs and NAFDAC.

What is the minimum order quantity for Abuja HoReCa buyers?

The minimum order is one pallet — approximately 400–500 kg of mixed SKUs. We allow flexible assortment selection so restaurant groups and catering companies can combine smoked beef, chicken and sausage lines in a single shipment. Volume discounts apply from 3 pallets and above.

Is Private Label available for Nigerian retail chains?

Yes. We offer full Private Label production starting from one pallet — your brand, your label design, our certified manufacturing. This service is well suited to supermarket chains operating in the FCT, as well as regional distributors looking to build a proprietary premium smoked meats range for the Nigerian market.

What shelf life do the smoked products have for the long sea transit to Lagos?

Shelf life ranges from 45 to 90 days depending on the SKU, under continuous refrigeration at 0–6°C. We offer vacuum and MAP (Modified Atmosphere Packaging) options specifically engineered for long-haul export routes. Production and dispatch are scheduled to ensure at least 60 days of remaining shelf life upon arrival in Lagos.
