FAQ – halal pelmeni for KL market

1. Is your halal certificate accepted by JAKIM or Malaysian authorities?

Our halal certificate (DUM RT #614A/2024) is issued by the Spiritual Board of Muslims of Tatarstan, which follows the same Shafi’i / Hanafi methodology. We regularly export to Southeast Asia and provide halal recognition letters for JAKIM-registered importers. Additional local halal endorsement can be arranged per buyer.

2. Do you offer samples for KL foodservice trials?

Yes. We ship frozen samples (5–10 kg) via air freight to Kuala Lumpur. You can test the pelmeni in your kitchen – boil, fry, or steam. We cover the sample cost; you pay logistics. Contact us for a trial order.

3. What is the minimum order quantity for retail or HoReCa?

For sea freight: 10 MT (mixed pallets of beef & chicken pelmeni). For air freight: 300 kg. Private label: min. 5 MT with your packaging design. We are flexible for first-time partners.

4. How do you guarantee no pork, no nitrite, and clean label?

Every batch is tested in an accredited lab. Our recipe contains only beef/chicken, flour, water, onions, salt, spices – zero nitrite, zero preservatives, zero GMO. We provide certificate of analysis and halal slaughter certificates for each lot.

5. Can you adapt the pelmeni taste for Malaysian palate?

Absolutely. We offer recipe adjustments – lower fat, local spices, or smaller dumpling size. Our R&D team works with your chef to create a custom profile. Minimum 2 MT for tailored recipe.
