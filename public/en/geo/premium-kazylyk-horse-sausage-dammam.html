FAQ — Dammam & Saudi Arabia

Questions we receive most often from Eastern Province buyers.

1. Is Premium Kazylyk certified halal for the Saudi Arabian market?

Yes. Kazylyk carries halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024, issued by the Muslim Spiritual Board of the Republic of Tatarstan. The product is 100% horse meat — no pork, no lard, no alcohol. We provide the original certificate plus an English translation for SFDA registration purposes.

2. How is Kazylyk delivered to Dammam and what are the transit times?

We offer two routes: sea freight via Novorossiysk → Jeddah (approx. 12 days), with onward trucking to Dammam via the King Fahd Causeway corridor; or air freight Kazan → Riyadh (approx. 5 hours flight). Terms are EXW Kazan; we assist with export documentation and can recommend trusted freight forwarders with Saudi experience.

3. What shelf life and cold-chain requirements apply during shipping?

Premium Kazylyk is a dry-cured product with a shelf life of up to 90 days at +2°C to +6°C. Vacuum-sealed packaging maintains integrity across the full transit duration. For air shipments we recommend chilled freight; for sea freight, refrigerated containers are standard for this product category.

4. Can Dammam restaurants and HoReCa operators order under their own brand?

Yes. Private-label (SBM) production is available: your brand name, Arabic and English labeling, custom spice profile, and packaging format. We already produce private-label products for clients including OMPK ("Aslam" brand) in Russia. Contact us to discuss your MOQ and specification.

5. What makes Kazylyk relevant for Saudi specialty restaurants and gourmet retail?

Kazylyk is a rare, authentic Central Asian delicacy — not a commodity. Dammam's growing premium food scene, international expat community, and curiosity-driven gourmet retail sector create a natural niche for a product that is genuinely unique: a centuries-old Tatar recipe, clean label, no nitrites, and a compelling origin story from the Muslim capital of Russia.
