Frequently Asked Questions — Mombasa & Kenya

Is your pepperoni certified halal for the Kenyan market?

Yes. Our pepperoni carries Halal certificate #614A/2024 issued by DUM RT — the Muslim Spiritual Board of the Republic of Tatarstan. The product contains zero pork and zero alcohol: 100% beef, chicken or horse meat. We can provide full halal documentation for Kenyan import authorities on request.

How long does sea freight from Kazan to Mombasa take?

Transit time by sea freight to Mombasa Port is 28–35 days depending on routing and transshipment hub. We ship EXW Kazan; your freight forwarder or our logistics partner handles the rest. Export packaging is specifically designed to maintain product integrity throughout this transit window.

What diameters and formats work best for Mombasa pizzerias and hotel kitchens?

Ø55 mm is the most popular format among our pizzeria clients for standard pizza toppings. Hotel chefs and upscale restaurants often prefer Ø65–75 mm for charcuterie presentations. We offer pre-sliced vacuum packs and whole sticks — the right format for your kitchen workflow is confirmed at the sampling stage.

Absolutely. We offer full Private Label manufacturing: your brand name, adjusted recipe, chosen casing diameter and export-compliant packaging. We already produce SBM lines for major retail and HoReCa networks. Minimum quantities and NDA terms are discussed individually.

What currencies and payment terms do you offer for Kenyan buyers?

We invoice in USD as standard for East African clients. Pricing is also available in AED for buyers routing through Dubai. Payment terms — prepayment or letter of credit — are agreed at the contract stage. Contact us for a commercial offer tailored to your import volumes.
