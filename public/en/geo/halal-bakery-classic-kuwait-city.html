Frequently Asked Questions — Kuwait City Buyers

Answers to the most common questions from Kuwaiti restaurant operators and food importers.

Is the halal certificate recognized by Kuwaiti food import authorities?

Yes. The Halal certificate issued by DUM RT (#614A/2024) is accepted by GCC Islamic certification bodies and aligns with the requirements of Kuwait's Ministry of Commerce and Industry for halal food imports. We provide the full original documentation with every shipment.

What is the minimum order for wholesale delivery to Kuwait City?

The minimum international order is one refrigerated pallet, approximately 500 kg of mixed or single-SKU product. For restaurant chains and distributors, we regularly handle full 20-foot reefer containers with consolidated assortments of pies, samsa, and chebureki.

How long does sea freight from Russia to Kuwait City take?

Transit from Novorossiysk to Kuwait City port is approximately 15 days in −18 °C reefer containers. Products arrive with a minimum of 10 months of remaining shelf life. We coordinate all export documentation including veterinary certificates for Kuwaiti customs.

Can you produce Private Label bakery under our Kuwaiti brand?

Absolutely. We offer full Private Label production with Arabic-language packaging, custom branding, and nutritional labeling formatted to Kuwait GSO standards. The minimum production run starts at 2 tonnes per SKU, making it accessible for mid-size Kuwaiti food brands.

Are the products suitable for Kuwait's health-conscious restaurant segment?

Yes. Our entire bakery line is a clean-label product: no sodium nitrite, no artificial colors, no GMO ingredients. This makes it well-suited for Kuwait's growing premium casual dining and health-oriented café segment, where menu transparency is increasingly expected by guests.
