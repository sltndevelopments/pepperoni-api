Frequently Asked Questions — Abuja & Nigeria

Do you supply halal-certified frozen baked goods to Abuja restaurants and fast-food outlets?

Yes. We supply frozen halal pies, samsa and chebureki to HoReCa operators in Abuja and across Nigeria. All products carry Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024 alongside HACCP and ISO 22000:2018 compliance — the documentation stack that serious restaurant chains and institutional caterers require.

How does sea freight delivery to Abuja work, and how long does it take?

We ship in refrigerated (reefer) containers from Russian ports to the Port of Lagos — transit time is 30–40 days. From Lagos, cargo is forwarded to Abuja via refrigerated road transport by our Nigerian logistics partner. We coordinate the full cold chain and provide tracking at every stage.

What is the minimum order quantity for wholesale baked goods for cafes and restaurants?

The minimum order is one consolidated pallet, approximately 500 kg. For new partners in Nigeria we offer a trial shipment option so your team can evaluate product quality, baking performance and shelf life before committing to a full container.

Is Private Label production available for Nigerian food brands?

Absolutely. We produce under Private Label with your brand name, logo and bilingual packaging (English + Arabic available). The minimum run is 1,000 kg per SKU. Several West African distributors already sell our products under their own brands in the Nigerian market.

What documentation do you provide for Nigerian customs and NAFDAC registration?

We prepare the full export documentation package: Russian veterinary certificates, Halal certificate DUM RT #614A/2024, HACCP and ISO 22000:2018 certificates, Certificate of Origin, TR CU 021/2011 conformity declaration, and a detailed product specification sheet. Our export team has prior experience supporting NAFDAC-registered importers.
