Frequently Asked Questions — Kuala Lumpur Market

Is your halal certification recognised in Malaysia under JAKIM standards?

Our halal certificate is issued by DUM RT (Tatarstan), a recognised Islamic authority. Malaysia's JAKIM maintains a list of approved foreign halal certification bodies. We recommend your local importer verify current recognition status directly with JAKIM — this is standard procedure for any imported halal product and takes 1–2 business days to confirm.

What is the minimum order quantity for export to Kuala Lumpur?

For sea freight, we typically work with LCL consolidations from 500 kg net weight, which keeps per-unit logistics costs competitive for distributors and mid-size food service operators. Air freight trial orders can be arranged from smaller volumes — contact us to discuss your specific requirements.

Can your products be listed in Malaysian hypermarkets such as Aeon or Lotus's?

Yes. Our HACCP and ISO 22000:2018 certifications meet the standard vendor audit criteria used by Malaysian hypermarket chains. Private Label production allows you to list products under your own brand, which simplifies the buyer approval process at retail level.

Do you provide Malay or English product labelling for the Malaysian market?

Yes, through our Private Label service we produce packaging with English and Bahasa Malaysia labelling, including nutritional information formatted to Malaysian Food Regulations 1985 requirements and halal logo placement as specified by the importer's halal authority.

How do your products perform in a high-volume fast-casual kitchen environment?

Our frozen pastries are designed for consistent results with minimal kitchen skill requirements — they can be baked or fried from frozen in 8–12 minutes. Portion weights are standardised to within ±5g, which supports cost control in high-volume outlets. Several KL food court operators use our samsa as a permanent menu item due to this consistency.
