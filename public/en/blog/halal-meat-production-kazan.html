Halal Raw Material Sourcing & Verification

Halal Meat Procurement Standards

Every kilogram of beef, chicken, and horse meat entering our facility undergoes halal verification:

  • Slaughter compliance: Animals slaughtered according to Islamic law with invocation of "Bismillah"
  • Documentation: Full traceability with halal certificates for each batch from approved suppliers
  • Prohibited substances: Absolute exclusion of pork, blood derivatives, alcohol-based ingredients, and non-halal animal enzymes

We source primarily from certified suppliers in Tatarstan and adjacent Russian regions, all audited by the Muslim Spiritual Board of the Republic of Tatarstan (MSB RT). This regional supply chain reduces logistics costs and ensures meat freshness — a critical factor in achieving superior taste and texture.

Horse Meat & Traditional Kazylyk

Among our signature products is kazylyk, a traditional Tatar air-dried horse meat sausage. Horse meat is lean, high in protein, and carries cultural significance in Tatar cuisine. For international distributors, kazylyk offers a differentiated product in ethnic food sections or specialty halal assortments.

Horse meat sourcing follows the same strict halal protocols as beef and poultry. Our kazylyk production uses artisanal dry-curing techniques refined over generations, making it both authentic and compliant with modern food safety standards.

Production Process: From Raw Material to Finished Product

Stage 1: Intake & Preparation

Chilled meat (0–4°C) arrives with accompanying halal documentation. Quality control inspects each delivery for temperature compliance, visual appearance, and paperwork accuracy. Meat undergoes deboning, trimming, and sorting by grade before entering production lines.

Stage 2: Grinding, Mixing & Curing

Production methodology varies by product category:

  • Cooked sausages and frankfurters: Fine emulsification in high-speed cutters with curing salt (within EU/EAEU regulatory limits)
  • Semi-smoked and smoked sausages: Coarse grinding, extended curing at controlled temperatures for flavor development
  • Dry-cured products (kazylyk): Traditional hand-stuffing, salt curing, and controlled humidity aging

All spice blends are proprietary recipes using halal-verified seasonings — garlic, black pepper, paprika, cumin — calibrated for each product's flavor profile.

Stage 3: Casing & Thermal Processing

We use both natural casings (beef intestines) and collagen casings depending on product specification. For food service applications like pizza pepperoni, calibrated collagen casings ensure uniform slicing and consistent portion control.

Product Type Processing Method Core Temp / Duration
Cooked sausages Steam cooking 72–80°C / 40–90 min
Semi-smoked Smoking + cooking 60–72°C / 2–4 hours
Dry-cured Cold smoking 18–22°C / 5–15 days
Kazylyk Air drying 8–12°C / 14–30 days

Stage 4: Cooling, Packaging & Storage

Finished products undergo rapid chilling to halt bacterial growth, then vacuum-packed or sealed in modified atmosphere packaging (MAP). MAP extends shelf life without chemical preservatives — critical for distribution networks with longer lead times.

All products are stored at <4°C and shipped in refrigerated transport to maintain cold chain integrity from our facility to your warehouse.
