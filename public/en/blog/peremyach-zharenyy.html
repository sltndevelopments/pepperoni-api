Fried Peremyach: Tatar Cheburek for Fast Food and Catering

Peremyach is a traditional Tatar deep-fried pastry with meat filling. In the HoReCa segment it is positioned as a halal alternative to cheburek — it has a different dough recipe and cooking method, providing consistent quality when batch fried. Suitable for fast food, catering, and as a hot appetizer for banquets.

What is Peremyach and How Does It Differ from Cheburek

Peremyach is a closed deep-fried pie made from yeast or plain dough, traditionally round in shape with a small hole in the center. Unlike cheburek, it uses yeast dough, the filling contains raw meat (not pre-cooked), and it is deep-fried rather than shallow-fried.

Key difference: The hole in the center allows the meat filling to cook through faster during deep frying, ensuring the meat is fully done by the time the dough is golden.

Dough Features

The classic peremyach dough is made from wheat flour, water, milk, eggs, butter or vegetable oil, salt, sugar, and yeast. The dough should be soft, elastic, and slightly stickier than for standard pies. After mixing, it must rest for 1-1.5 hours. Yeast dough gives the finished product an airy texture and characteristic fluffiness.

Filling and Preparation

The classic filling is halal ground beef with onions. Some recipes allow chicken or mixed meat. The meat is ground with onions, seasoned with salt, black pepper, and sometimes cumin. The ground meat is left uncooked and placed into the dough raw. The formed peremyach is deep-fried at 160-170°C for 5-7 minutes until golden brown.

Commercial Advantages for HoReCa

For catering and fast food establishments, peremyach offers several advantages: consistent portion size (80-100 g), quick preparation from frozen state (3-5 minutes in the oven), longer shelf life frozen (up to 6 months), and a unique menu item in the halal category. Wholesale suppliers offer frozen semi-finished peremyach that can be fried as needed.
