Private Label Sausage Production: How OEM Manufacturing Works

Private Label (OEM) in the meat industry is the production of sausage products under the customer's brand. Retailers, HoReCa chains, and distributors gain the ability to sell products with their own label, controlling margins and building customer loyalty. In 2026, the share of Private Label in the meat category reached 28% of the Russian market, making this format particularly relevant for business development.

What is Private Label in Sausage Production

Private Label is contract manufacturing where the producer makes products according to the customer's specifications but under the customer's trademark. In the sausage industry, this can be pepperoni for pizza, cooked sausages, frankfurters, poultry or beef delicacies.

The key difference from regular wholesale: the customer receives not finished products in the manufacturer's branded packaging, but goods with their own label design, composition, and positioning. The manufacturer handles the full cycle: from recipe development to packaging of finished goods. For businesses, this means the ability to create a unique offering without investing in their own production facilities.

Advantages of Private Label for HoReCa and Retail

The first advantage is increased margins. Products under your own brand allow pricing without being tied to well-known competitor brands. According to statistics, margins on Private Label products are 15-25% higher compared to reselling branded products.

The second is quality and composition control. The customer participates in recipe development, can require halal certification, exclude certain ingredients, or add functional components. This is especially important for pizzerias with specific requirements for sausage products.

The third is brand building. Your own trademark increases business recognition, builds customer trust, and reduces dependence on suppliers. The customer buys not just sausage but a product from a specific chain or restaurant, strengthening loyalty.

Stages of Launching Private Label Production

Stage 1: Concept development. Defining the target audience, price segment, and assortment. Decide which types of sausages will be produced: cooked, semi-smoked, wholesale pepperoni for pizza, or delicacies. Study demand and competitors.

Stage 2: Choosing a manufacturer. Selection criteria: certifications (HACCP, halal if needed), production capacity, experience with Private Label, recipe flexibility, minimum order quantity. The manufacturer should provide samples and technical documentation.

Stage 3: Recipe creation. The manufacturer's technologist works with the customer to develop the composition, considering requirements for taste, texture, and shelf life. Test batches and tastings are conducted until the final version is approved.

Stage 4: Legal formalities. Trademark registration, label development in compliance with TR CU 022/2011, production contract signing. The manufacturer arranges the declaration of conformity for the customer's products.

Stage 5: Production and logistics. Serial production launch, quality control, packaging, labeling. Organizing delivery to points of sale with temperature compliance of +2...+6°C.

Economics and Minimum Order Quantities

The minimum batch for launching Private Label depends on the manufacturer and product type. For cooked sausages, this is typically from 500 kg; for pepperoni and semi-smoked products, from 300 kg per production cycle. Launch investments include label design (15,000-30,000 RUB), trademark registration (20,000-35,000 RUB), and the first production batch.

The cost of Private Label products is on average 20-30% lower than the purchase price of branded analogs of the same quality. The customer can set retail prices at the level of well-known brands, earning additional margin. Project payback with stable sales occurs within 4-8 months.

An important point is volume planning. The manufacturer works most efficiently with regular orders, so it's optimal to establish a delivery schedule considering product turnover. For HoReCa chains, the format of weekly or bi-weekly small-batch deliveries with a fixed assortment is convenient.

Practical Recommendations for Launching Private Label

  • Start with one or two high-demand items — such as cooked sausage and pepperoni. This reduces risk and allows market testing.
  • Require quality certificates and laboratory test results for each batch. Your brand's reputation depends on product consistency.
  • Invest in packaging design. An attractive label increases sales conversion by 35-40%. Use quality printing and informative ingredient descriptions.
  • Include exclusivity terms in the contract for your region's recipe, preventing the manufacturer from making identical products for competitors.
  • Organize customer feedback and adjust the product as needed. Flexibility is the key advantage of Private Label.
  • Plan promotion: tastings, promotions, prominent shelf placement. Private Label requires active marketing at launch.

Conclusion

Private Label in sausage production is an effective tool for increasing profits and building your own brand. Choosing the right manufacturer, quality control, and competent marketing allow creating a sought-after product line with high margins. In an environment of growing competition, Private Label offers flexibility, unique offerings, and customer loyalty.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Private Label in sausage production?

Private Label is contract manufacturing of sausages under the customer's brand. The manufacturer produces products according to the client's specifications, including recipe and packaging, but the brand belongs to the customer.

How is Private Label more profitable for business than reselling brands?

Margins on Private Label products are 15-25% higher compared to reselling. The customer controls prices, quality, and product composition while building loyalty to their own brand.

Can I order halal sausage through Private Label?

Yes, by choosing a manufacturer with halal certification, you can include this requirement in the specification. The customer participates in recipe development and can require compliance with halal standards.

How many stages does launching a Private Label sausage brand involve?

Launching an OEM brand includes at least 4 stages: concept and assortment development, selecting a manufacturer with required certificates, creating and testing recipes, legal registration and trademark filing.
