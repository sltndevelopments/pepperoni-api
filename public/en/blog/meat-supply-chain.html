Wholesale Meat Products: How to Build a Supply Chain

An effective meat supply chain synchronizes production, logistics, and quality control at every stage. Proper process organization ensures assortment stability, reduces losses, and guarantees product safety for the end consumer. This article covers key elements of building a reliable supply system for restaurants, cafes, and retail outlets.

Supplier Selection: Manufacturer Evaluation Criteria

The supply chain foundation is laid when selecting a manufacturer. The first criterion is valid conformity certificates and veterinary documents. Production must operate under HACCP, confirming critical control points at all manufacturing stages.

Request information about raw material suppliers. Meat origin traceability is critical for halal products and enables quick response to potential issues. Check production capacity: a stable supplier has backup lines and can meet volumes even during peak seasons.

Evaluate geographic location. A plant in your region reduces delivery time and logistics risks. For example, our production in Kazan ensures product freshness for clients in Tatarstan and neighboring regions.

Tip: Before signing a contract, order a trial batch and conduct tasting. Organoleptic indicators — taste, smell, texture — should remain stable from batch to batch.
