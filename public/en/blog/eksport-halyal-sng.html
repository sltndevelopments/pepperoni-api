Halal Product Export to CIS Countries: Kazakhstan, Belarus, Uzbekistan

Export of halal meat products to CIS countries opens access for Russian manufacturers to a market of over 30 million Muslims. Kazakhstan, Belarus and Uzbekistan actively import certified halal products, with documentation and logistics requirements varying by destination. This article covers all key export aspects: from obtaining certificates to organizing supplies and finding buyers.

Halal Product Market in CIS Countries

CIS countries demonstrate steady growth in demand for certified halal products. Kazakhstan, where Muslims make up over 70% of the population, imports about 15% of its meat products, a significant share being halal. Uzbekistan, with a 28-million predominantly Muslim population, develops its own production but continues to purchase premium categories.

Belarus, despite a small Muslim population, is an important transit hub and consumer of halal products for food service and retail chains. Russian producers have a competitive advantage due to geographic proximity, the single EAEU customs space (for Kazakhstan and Belarus), and recognition of Russian halal certificates.
