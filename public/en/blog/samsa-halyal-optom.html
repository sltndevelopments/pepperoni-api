Halal Samsa with Chicken and Beef: Wholesale Supply

Halal samsa with chicken and beef is a popular item for cafes, canteens, bakeries, and food service chains catering to Muslim customers. Wholesale samsa with halal certification ensures consistent quality, religious compliance, and a ready-to-sell product with high margins. This article covers supplier selection criteria, production requirements, and logistics of halal samsa for professional kitchens.

Production Requirements for Halal Samsa

Production of samsa with halal labeling is regulated by Sharia standards and monitored by certifying bodies. Key requirements include:

  • Use of meat from animals slaughtered according to Islamic canons (with the name of Allah invoked, jugular vein cut).
  • Complete absence of prohibited ingredients: blood, gelatin of uncontrolled origin, alcohol.
  • Separate production of halal and regular products, or equipment cleaning between cycles.
  • Certification of all raw material suppliers — meat, spices, fats — for halal compliance.
  • Constant supervision by an accredited expert or religious body.

We produce samsa at a certified facility accredited by the Spiritual Administration of Muslims of the Republic of Tatarstan. All documentation is available for inspection by wholesale buyers.

Samsa Filling: Chicken or Beef

The two main types of meat filling for samsa — chicken and beef — differ in structure, taste, and cost. Chicken filling is more tender, cooks faster, has lower cost, and suits the mass segment. Beef samsa has a richer flavor, denser texture, and is considered the classic Central Asian option.

Our product line includes both options with 100% meat filling (no offal or soy protein). Standard composition: chopped meat, onions, tail fat, salt, black pepper, cumin. Recipe adaptation to client requirements is available — we can adjust seasoning intensity, fat content, and product size.

Storage and Preparation

Frozen samsa is supplied at -18°C with a shelf life of up to 90 days. Preparation: bake in a convection oven at 180-200°C for 20-25 minutes without pre-thawing. This format is convenient for establishments without their own dough preparation line — simply take from the freezer and bake.

For catering, we recommend supplying baked samsa that only needs reheating for 5-7 minutes. This saves time at the venue and ensures consistent product quality regardless of the cook's skill level.

Supplier Selection Criteria

When choosing a samsa supplier for a restaurant or cafe, consider: availability of valid halal certification, production location and conditions, consistency of product characteristics, minimum batch, delivery logistics, and willingness to customize recipes. It is recommended to order a trial batch of 10-20 kg for tasting and evaluating product quality before concluding a long-term contract.
