Sliced Pepperoni vs Whole Stick: What to Choose for HoReCa

For high-traffic establishments, ready-sliced pepperoni is optimal — saving up to 40 minutes of staff time per shift, consistent quality, and no waste. For small cafes and pizzerias, buying whole sticks is more profitable: control over slice thickness, 15-20% savings on purchase, and flexibility in product use. The choice depends on production volume, staff qualifications, and menu specifics of your establishment.

The Economics: Calculating Real Benefits

The price of ready slices is 15-25% higher per kilogram compared to whole stick. A whole pepperoni stick weighing 500-700 grams costs on average 800 rubles per kilogram wholesale, sliced — 920-1000 rubles. The difference seems significant, but hidden costs must be considered.

Manual slicing of sticks generates 5-7% waste: irregular end pieces, casing trimmings, uneven slices. For every kilogram of stick, 50-70 grams of product is lost. A chef spends 15-20 minutes slicing one kilogram including workspace preparation and slicer cleaning. At a salary of 250 rubles per hour, this is 60-80 rubles in labor costs per kilogram.

Ready slices are fully ready to use. Vacuum packaging preserves product freshness, eliminates contact with air and foreign odors. Staff open the package and immediately place slices on pizza or in salad. Time savings are critical during peak hours when every minute affects guest service speed.

Expert tip: For large pizzerias with more than 100 pizzas per day, ready slices pay off through time savings and reduced defect rates. Small establishments doing up to 30-40 pizzas benefit more from working with sticks.

Shelf Life and Delivery Logistics

Whole stick pepperoni in vacuum packaging keeps up to 120 days at 0...+6°C without loss of organoleptic properties. This allows creating a strategic reserve. Ready slicing in modified atmosphere packaging keeps 30-45 days depending on manufacturer technology. After opening, stick stays fresh 7-10 days, sliced — 3-5 days.

For logistics management, it is important to consider that whole sticks take up 30-40% less volume in the refrigerator than the equivalent amount of sliced product in vacuum packaging. For establishments with limited storage space, this can be a decisive factor.
