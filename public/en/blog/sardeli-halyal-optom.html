Halal Sausages Wholesale: How They Differ from Frankfurters

Sausages differ from frankfurters in size, composition, and production technology: they are larger (diameter 32-44 mm vs 18-24 mm), contain more meat (60-70% vs 40-50%), and have a denser texture. Halal sausages are made from beef, chicken, or turkey in compliance with Islamic norms, making them a sought-after product for restaurants, cafes, and canteens with diverse audiences. When purchasing wholesale, it is important to consider these differences for proper menu planning and portion calculation.

Main Differences Between Sausages and Frankfurters

Despite external similarity, sausages and frankfurters are different categories of cooked sausage products with their own technical characteristics. Understanding these differences is critical for HoReCa product buyers, as it affects dish costs, cooking methods, and guest perception.

Size and Weight

Sausages have a casing diameter of 32-44 mm and length of 10-12 cm, giving an average weight of 80-120 g per piece. Frankfurters are thinner — diameter 18-24 mm, length 12-15 cm, weight 40-60 g. For food service establishments, this means a difference in portion yield: one sausage can be a standalone main dish with a side, while frankfurters are usually served 2-3 per portion.

Composition and Meat Content

Premium and top-grade halal sausages contain 60-70% meat — beef, chicken, turkey, or their combinations. The composition includes fat, spices (black pepper, coriander, nutmeg), salt, nitrite curing mixture. Frankfurters, especially standard grade, contain 40-50% meat with added plant protein, starch, and carrageenan for moisture retention.

Higher meat content in sausages provides rich flavor, dense texture, and better nutritional value. When choosing between sausages and frankfurters for a restaurant menu, consider the target audience and positioning. Sausages are better suited for main courses and grilling, frankfurters for quick snacks and hot dogs.

Cooking Applications

Halal sausages are versatile in cooking: they can be boiled, fried, grilled, baked, or added to soups and casseroles. Due to their larger size, they work well on breakfast plates, as a hot appetizer, or as a component of main dishes. Frankfurters are primarily used for hot dogs, quick snacks, and children's menus.

Benefits for HoReCa

For restaurants and cafes, halal sausages offer higher yield per portion, better presentation on the plate, and wider culinary applications. Wholesale purchasing from a manufacturer allows significant cost reduction per serving while maintaining quality standards.
