Traditional Heritage Meets Modern Food Safety

The name "kazylyk" derives from the Turkic word "kaz" (goose), referencing the distinctive curved shape of this traditional sausage. For centuries, nomadic peoples of the Eurasian steppes have produced horse meat delicacies, and kazylyk remains one of the most celebrated expressions of this culinary heritage.

Horse meat offers a unique nutritional profile with lower fat content and higher protein density compared to beef or pork, making it increasingly attractive to health-conscious consumers. The meat's naturally sweet, slightly gamey flavor creates a distinctive taste experience that differentiates menu offerings and retail assortments.

Kazan Delicacies produces kazylyk according to HACCP standards while maintaining traditional preparation methods. Each sausage is crafted from carefully selected horse meat, seasoned with authentic spice blends, and naturally cured to develop the characteristic flavor and texture that defines premium kazylyk.
