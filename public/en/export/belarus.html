Certification & market access

A mutual-recognition memorandum applies: the halal certificate of the Muslim Board of Tatarstan (No. 614A/2024) is accepted in Belarus.

The facility (No. RU-016/SB88273) is listed in the Rosselkhoznadzor “Cerberus” registry and attested for export to Belarus.

Consumer demand for halal food products in Belarus has been growing steadily, driven by an expanding Muslim community, rising interest in ethically produced food among the broader population, and increasing tourism and business flows from halal-observant countries. Retail buyers in Minsk and regional centres such as Brest and Grodno are actively expanding dedicated halal sections, creating a clear opportunity for importers who can offer a wide, consistent product range.

HoReCa is one of the most dynamic segments: pizzerias and fast-food outlets across Belarus require halal pepperoni and sausages that meet both taste expectations and religious requirements. Burger concepts, shawarma chains, and catering companies in Gomel and beyond are looking for reliable suppliers of halal burger patties and smoked sausages. Authentic Tatar products — kazylyk, echpochmak, chak-chak — represent a differentiated premium niche with strong storytelling potential for specialty retail and food-service menus.

Sourcing from Kazan offers Belarusian buyers a distinct logistical and commercial advantage. Tatarstan shares deep halal food traditions and manufacturing expertise, resulting in products that are genuinely rooted in halal culture rather than simply compliant on paper. The geographic proximity within the post-Soviet trade space means competitive pricing, shorter transit times compared to Middle Eastern or Southeast Asian suppliers, and well-established cross-border freight infrastructure.

How supply works

Shipments from our production facility in Kazan to Belarus are carried out by refrigerated transport (0°C to +4°C for chilled products, −18°C for frozen lines), ensuring full cold-chain integrity throughout the journey. Transit time to Minsk typically ranges from two to four business days depending on the chosen carrier and customs clearance schedule. Our product shelf lives are designed for export: chilled sausages and pepperoni maintain quality for several weeks, while frozen burger patties, smoked sausages, and kazylyk offer extended storage windows that comfortably accommodate warehousing and distribution cycles within Belarus.

We work with importers on a pallet-based minimum order structure, with overall volumes starting from 500 kg per month — scalable as your distribution grows. To initiate a supply relationship, we ask partners to share their target product mix, required volumes, delivery frequency, and preferred Incoterms. Our export team prepares a commercial offer, product samples, and all necessary accompanying documentation within a short turnaround. We are open to both regular scheduled deliveries and consolidated mixed-product shipments tailored to your warehouse and sales cycle.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export to Belarus?

Our standard MOQ for export supply is 500 kg per month. This can be a single product or a mixed assortment — for example, a combination of halal pepperoni, hot-dog sausages, and burger patties. For first trial orders we are willing to discuss slightly smaller quantities to allow you to test the product with your customers before committing to regular volumes.

What documents do you provide with each shipment?

Each shipment is accompanied by a full commercial package: invoice, packing list, veterinary health certificate, country-of-origin document, and halal certificate issued by an accredited body. All paperwork is prepared in formats accepted for import into Belarus and is provided in advance of dispatch so your customs broker can begin clearance procedures without delay.

How long does delivery from Kazan to Belarus take, and how is the cold chain maintained?

Transit from our Kazan facility to Minsk or other major Belarusian cities typically takes two to four business days by refrigerated truck. We work with experienced freight partners who operate temperature-controlled vehicles throughout the entire route. For frozen product lines, transport is maintained at −18°C; chilled products travel at 0°C to +4°C. Temperature logs are available upon request.

Do you offer Private Label (own-brand) production for the Belarusian market?

Yes. We offer Private Label manufacturing starting from 500 kg per month per SKU. You provide your brand name, label design, and packaging specifications — we handle production, packaging, and export documentation under your brand. This option is popular with retail chains and distributors who want to build a proprietary halal product line without investing in their own production. Lead time for the first Private Label batch is typically four to six weeks from artwork approval.
