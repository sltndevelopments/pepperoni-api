🇪🇬 Halal Meat Products Export from Kazan, Russia to Egypt

Authentic Tatar halal craftsmanship — delivered to Cairo, Alexandria and beyond.

HALAL · B2B · EG

Kazan Delicacies (pepperoni.tatar) is a B2B halal meat manufacturer based in Kazan, Tatarstan — the heart of Russia's Islamic culinary tradition. We produce a full range of halal beef and poultry products: pepperoni, hot-dog sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, kazylyk (traditional Tatar dried sausage), and authentic Tatar baked goods including echpochmak and chak-chak. Every product is made exclusively from beef and poultry in a dedicated halal production environment.

We supply Egyptian importers, food distributors, retail chains, and HoReCa operators looking for a reliable, consistent source of premium halal meat products. Whether you are building a private label line, stocking a supermarket chain in Cairo, or supplying quick-service restaurants in Alexandria, we are ready to discuss a long-term wholesale partnership starting from 500 kg per month.

Certification & market access

The Halal Committee of the Muslim Board of Tatarstan (No. 614A/2024) holds international SMIIC accreditation. Certificate recognition in Egypt is confirmed individually when arranging a shipment.

The facility is listed in the Rosselkhoznadzor “Cerberus” registry (No. RU-016/SB88273); veterinary terms for Egypt are confirmed individually per contract.

Egypt is one of the largest halal food markets in the Middle East and North Africa, with a well-established culture of halal consumption across all segments — from household retail to fast-growing QSR and full-service restaurant sectors. Cairo and Alexandria together represent a significant concentration of food importers, HORECA operators, and modern trade networks actively seeking diversified, quality-assured halal protein sources.

Demand for processed halal meat formats — pepperoni for pizza chains, ready-to-grill burger patties for burger concepts, smoked sausages and hot dogs for catering — is growing steadily as Egypt's food service industry expands and consumer expectations rise. Specialty ethnic products such as kazylyk and traditional Tatar pastries present an additional niche opportunity for premium delicatessen retailers and ethnic food distributors targeting Egypt's diverse urban population.

Sourcing from Tatarstan, Russia offers Egyptian buyers a compelling combination: competitive ex-works pricing relative to Western European or Gulf-produced alternatives, a deeply rooted halal food culture backed by centuries of tradition, and well-established logistics corridors connecting Kazan to Black Sea and Caspian transit routes onward to Egypt. This translates into reliable supply cycles and cost-efficient landed prices for your business.

How supply works

Shipments from Kazan to Egypt are handled via refrigerated (0°C to +4°C) or frozen (−18°C) transport depending on the product category, ensuring full cold-chain integrity from our facility to the port of destination. Our processed meat products carry shelf lives that comfortably accommodate transit times along established routing through Turkish or Georgian transit hubs to Egyptian ports including Alexandria and Damietta. We work with experienced freight forwarders familiar with Russian export and Egyptian import procedures, and can coordinate full-pallet or full-container loads to match your order volume and frequency.

To initiate a supply relationship, we ask prospective Egyptian partners to share their target product list, estimated monthly volumes, preferred delivery terms (EXW, CIF, or DAP), and any specific packaging or labeling requirements for the Egyptian market. Our export team will prepare a tailored commercial offer, product specifications, and sample availability within a short turnaround. We are open to discussing exclusive distribution arrangements for defined territories within Egypt for partners committing to regular minimum volumes.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export to Egypt?

Our standard MOQ for export orders is 500 kg per month across a single product or a mixed assortment. For initial trial orders, we can discuss smaller pilot shipments to allow you to assess product quality and market reception before committing to full commercial volumes. Please contact our export team to discuss the most suitable arrangement for your business.

What documents do you provide for customs clearance in Egypt?

We provide a full standard export documentation package including a commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading or CMR, veterinary health certificate issued by the competent Russian authority, and a certificate of origin. We work closely with our export logistics partners to ensure all paperwork meets Egyptian import requirements. Specific additional documentation requests can be accommodated — please discuss your requirements with us in advance.

What are the typical lead times from order confirmation to delivery in Egypt?

Lead time depends on whether goods are shipped from existing stock or produced to order. For in-stock items, production and preparation for shipment typically takes 5–10 business days. Transit time from Kazan to Egyptian ports via refrigerated road or multimodal routing generally ranges from 10 to 20 days depending on the routing and customs clearance speed. We will provide you with a specific timeline estimate at the quotation stage.

Do you offer Private Label (white label) production for the Egyptian market?

Yes. We offer Private Label manufacturing for Egyptian distributors and retail chains starting from 500 kg per month per SKU. We can produce under your brand name with custom packaging, Arabic-language labeling, and tailored product specifications (spice profiles, portion weights, packaging formats) adapted to Egyptian consumer and regulatory requirements. Our team will guide you through the development and approval process from concept to first shipment.
