Kazan Delicacies (pepperoni.tatar) is a Russian halal meat manufacturer based in Kazan, Tatarstan — a region with deep-rooted halal food traditions and a well-developed production infrastructure. We produce a full range of ready-to-use halal products: pepperoni, hot dog sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, kazylyk, and authentic Tatar pastries including echpochmak and chak-chak — all made exclusively from beef and poultry.

Whether you are sourcing for a network of pizzerias in Tbilisi, supplying hotel kitchens in Batumi, or building a private-label halal brand for the Georgian retail market, we offer consistent quality, flexible batch sizes starting from 500 kg per month, and a dedicated export team to support your procurement process.

Certification & market access

Products carry the halal certificate of the Muslim Board of Tatarstan (No. 614A/2024) with international SMIIC and GSO/GCC accreditation; recognition terms in Georgia are confirmed when arranging supply.

The facility (No. RU-016/SB88273) is listed in the Rosselkhoznadzor “Cerberus” registry and attested for export to Georgia.

Georgia is home to a growing and diverse food service culture, with Tbilisi and Batumi attracting international visitors, a rising middle class, and an expanding network of modern retail and hospitality businesses. Demand for high-quality, reliably sourced halal meat products is increasing across multiple segments — from fast-food chains and burger restaurants to hotel buffets, catering companies, and specialty grocery retailers serving Muslim communities and health-conscious consumers alike.

Pizzerias and QSR operators across Georgia increasingly seek halal-certified pepperoni and smoked toppings as a way to expand their customer base without compromising on taste or quality. At the same time, Georgian retail chains and importers are beginning to develop dedicated halal sections, creating real shelf space for products that combine European food safety standards with authentic halal production. Tatar specialties such as kazylyk and chak-chak also represent a differentiated, high-margin niche for specialty food retailers and ethnic grocery distributors.

Sourcing from Kazan offers Georgian buyers a practical advantage: Tatarstan has one of the most established halal food industries in Russia, combining competitive production costs, strong raw material supply chains, and genuine cultural expertise in halal cuisine. Geographic proximity compared to Middle Eastern or Southeast Asian suppliers, combined with well-established road and multimodal freight routes through the South Caucasus corridor, makes Kazan a logical and cost-efficient sourcing point for the Georgian market.

How supply works

All shipments from Kazan to Georgia are carried out in temperature-controlled refrigerated trucks, ensuring the cold chain is maintained from our production facility to your warehouse in Tbilisi, Batumi, or any other destination city. Our frozen and chilled product lines carry shelf lives that comfortably accommodate transit times along this route. We dispatch consolidated or full pallets depending on order volume, with minimum order quantities starting from one pallet per SKU. Our export team coordinates all necessary shipping documentation and works with experienced freight forwarders familiar with Georgian customs requirements.

To initiate a supply partnership, we ask prospective buyers to provide their company registration details, a target product list with approximate monthly volumes, and preferred delivery terms (EXW, DAP, or as agreed). We will then prepare a commercial offer within two business days. For new partners, we recommend starting with a trial shipment to validate product fit for your market before committing to a regular supply schedule. Our account managers are available in English to support you throughout the onboarding process.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export to Georgia?

Our standard MOQ for export orders is 500 kg per month across the product range. For individual SKUs, we recommend a minimum of one pallet to optimize freight costs. We are open to discussing flexible arrangements for new partners during an initial trial period.

What commercial and shipping documents do you provide with each shipment?

We provide a full export documentation package with every shipment, including a commercial invoice, packing list, veterinary health certificate, and a certificate of origin. All documents are prepared in accordance with Georgian import requirements. Our export team will advise you on any additional documentation specific to your customs broker's needs.

How long does delivery from Kazan to Georgia typically take?

Transit time from our facility in Kazan to Tbilisi or Batumi is generally between 5 and 10 business days depending on the chosen logistics route and border crossing conditions. We work with established freight partners experienced in South Caucasus deliveries to ensure reliable scheduling. Exact lead times are confirmed at the order stage.

Do you offer Private Label (own-brand) production for the Georgian market?

Yes. We offer full Private Label manufacturing for distributors and retail chains who wish to sell our products under their own brand in Georgia. This includes custom packaging design, labeling in the required language, and tailored product specifications. The minimum volume for Private Label programs is typically 500 kg per month per SKU. Please contact our export team to discuss your requirements and receive a dedicated proposal.
