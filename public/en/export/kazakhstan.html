Authentic Tatar halal production — delivered to Almaty, Astana, Shymkent and beyond.

Kazan Delicacies (pepperoni.tatar) is a B2B-focused manufacturer of premium halal meat and bakery products based in Kazan, Republic of Tatarstan, Russia. Our product range includes halal pepperoni, hot-dog sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, kazylyk, and traditional Tatar baked goods such as echpochmak and chak-chak — all produced from beef and poultry under strict halal standards.

We supply importers, regional distributors, retail chains, and HoReCa operators across Kazakhstan. Whether you are sourcing for a pizza chain in Almaty, a supermarket network in Astana, or a fast-food operator in Shymkent or Karaganda, we offer reliable wholesale volumes, Private Label (OEM) production from 500 kg per month, and a dedicated export manager to support your procurement process end to end.

Certification & market access

A mutual-recognition memorandum applies: the halal certificate of the Muslim Board of Tatarstan (No. 614A/2024) is accepted in Kazakhstan.

The facility (No. RU-016/SB88273) is listed in the Rosselkhoznadzor “Cerberus” registry and attested for export to Kazakhstan.

Kazakhstan represents one of the most strategically important halal food markets in Central Asia. With a predominantly Muslim population and a rapidly growing food service sector, demand for consistently high-quality halal meat products — from ready-to-cook burger patties to premium smoked sausages — is expanding across all major cities. Retailers in Almaty and Astana are actively seeking differentiated halal SKUs, while the HoReCa segment in Shymkent and Karaganda is scaling up demand for portion-ready and processed meat formats.

Pizzerias and fast-food concepts throughout Kazakhstan have a particular need for halal pepperoni and sausage toppings that meet both taste expectations and religious compliance requirements. Traditional Tatar products such as kazylyk and echpochmak also resonate strongly in the Kazakhstani market, where shared culinary heritage creates immediate consumer recognition and shelf appeal.

Sourcing from Kazan offers Kazakhstani buyers a compelling combination of geographic proximity, competitive pricing, and deep-rooted halal production culture. Tatarstan has centuries of Islamic culinary tradition, and our manufacturing reflects that heritage in every product. Compared to imports from more distant origins, the Russia–Kazakhstan supply corridor via the EAEU framework provides simplified customs procedures, shorter transit times, and lower logistics overhead — advantages that directly benefit your margins and inventory planning.

How supply works

Deliveries from Kazan to Kazakhstan are carried out by refrigerated transport (reefer trucks), maintaining the cold chain throughout the journey. Transit time to Almaty or Astana typically ranges from three to six days depending on route and border conditions. Our products are manufactured with shelf lives suitable for international distribution, giving your warehouse and sales teams adequate time for receiving, quality control, and in-market placement. Minimum shipment starts from one pallet, with full truck loads available for established partners. We work with experienced freight forwarders familiar with the EAEU cross-border requirements, ensuring smooth customs clearance on the Kazakhstani side.

To initiate a supply relationship, we ask prospective partners to share their product category needs, estimated monthly volumes, and preferred delivery destination (city or warehouse address). Our export team will prepare a tailored commercial offer including product specifications, pricing tiers, lead times, and packaging options. For Private Label orders, we require brand artwork and label specifications at least three weeks before the first production run. We recommend starting with a trial shipment to validate product performance in your market before committing to larger standing orders.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export to Kazakhstan?

Our standard MOQ for export orders is 500 kg per month across the product mix. For single SKUs such as halal pepperoni or smoked sausages, we can discuss pallet-level minimums. Larger volumes unlock tiered pricing — contact our export team to receive a volume-based quote tailored to your needs.

What shipping and export documents do you provide with each order?

Each shipment is accompanied by a full set of commercial export documents, including the commercial invoice, packing list, veterinary health certificate, and country-of-origin certificate. All paperwork is prepared in formats accepted for EAEU cross-border trade and Kazakhstani customs clearance. Our logistics coordinator works with you or your customs broker to ensure a smooth import process.

How long does delivery from Kazan to major Kazakhstani cities take?

Refrigerated road freight from Kazan to Almaty or Astana typically takes between three and six business days. Delivery to Shymkent or Karaganda falls within a similar window. We schedule production and dispatch to align with your required arrival dates, and we provide shipment tracking details so your receiving team can plan accordingly.

Do you offer Private Label (OEM) production for Kazakhstani distributors or retail chains?

Yes. We offer full Private Label manufacturing starting from 500 kg per month per SKU. You provide your brand name, label design, and any specific recipe or packaging requirements, and we handle production, labeling, and export preparation. Lead time for the first Private Label run is approximately four to five weeks from artwork approval. This service is popular with regional retail chains and HoReCa operators looking to build proprietary halal meat brands in the Kazakhstani market.
