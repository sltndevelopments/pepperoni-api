Authentic Tatar halal production — delivered to Bishkek, Osh and beyond.

HALAL · B2B · KG

Kazan Delicacies (pepperoni.tatar) is a B2B manufacturer of halal meat and food products based in Kazan, Tatarstan, Russia. We produce a full range of beef and poultry-based items — from pepperoni and hot-dog sausages to smoked sausages, burger patties, kazylyk, and traditional Tatar baked goods including echpochmak and chak-chak — all manufactured under strict halal standards at every stage of production.

We supply importers, regional distributors, retail chains, and HoReCa operators across Kyrgyzstan. Whether you are sourcing for a supermarket network in Bishkek, a restaurant group in Osh, or building a private-label product line, we offer flexible wholesale terms starting from 500 kg per month with consistent quality and reliable cross-border logistics.

Certification & market access

The halal certificate of the Muslim Board of Tatarstan (No. 614A/2024) is directly recognized in Kyrgyzstan.

The facility (No. RU-016/SB88273) is listed in the Rosselkhoznadzor “Cerberus” registry and attested for export to Kyrgyzstan.

Kyrgyzstan is a predominantly Muslim country where halal food is not a niche segment but a mainstream consumer expectation. Demand for high-quality processed halal meat products — ready-to-cook items, deli meats, and fast-food components — is growing steadily across both urban centers. Bishkek's expanding modern retail sector and Osh's strong food-service culture represent two distinct and complementary distribution channels for imported halal products.

The HoReCa segment in Kyrgyzstan presents a strong opportunity for foodservice-grade halal pepperoni (pizza toppings), burger patties, and hot-dog sausages as international fast-food formats and local café chains continue to develop. Retail buyers and wholesalers are increasingly looking for consistent, branded or private-label halal chilled and frozen SKUs that meet the expectations of quality-conscious urban consumers.

Sourcing from Tatarstan carries a natural cultural and commercial advantage: Kazan has centuries of halal food tradition, and Tatar producers are well understood and trusted across Central Asia. Geographical proximity compared to more distant suppliers, shared transport corridors, and competitive ex-works pricing make Kazan a practical and cost-effective origin for Kyrgyz importers seeking to upgrade their halal meat assortment.

How supply works

Shipments from our facility in Kazan to Kyrgyzstan are carried out by refrigerated road transport (reefer trucks) operating along established freight corridors through Kazakhstan to Bishkek and Osh. Chilled products typically reach Bishkek within 3–5 transit days; frozen product lines allow greater scheduling flexibility. Our product shelf lives are designed for export: frozen items provide ample time for customs clearance, warehousing, and retail distribution without compromise to quality. We work with experienced freight partners familiar with Kyrgyz customs procedures and can coordinate door-to-warehouse delivery or work with your nominated carrier.

To initiate a supply relationship, a prospective Kyrgyz partner typically provides their company details and target product list so we can prepare a tailored commercial offer and sample shipment. Minimum order quantities start from one pallet per SKU or a consolidated mixed pallet from 500 kg total. We support new partners through the onboarding process — including product specifications, labeling requirements, and packaging customization for private-label orders — to ensure a smooth first delivery and long-term cooperation.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export to Kyrgyzstan?

Our export MOQ starts at 500 kg per month. This can be a single product or a consolidated mixed order across several SKUs — for example, pepperoni, smoked sausages, and burger patties combined. For private-label orders, we discuss volume thresholds individually depending on the product category and packaging requirements.

How long does delivery from Kazan to Bishkek or Osh take?

Refrigerated road freight from Kazan to Bishkek typically takes 3 to 5 transit days under normal conditions. Delivery to Osh may require an additional 1–2 days. For frozen product lines, scheduling is more flexible and we can align dispatch dates with your warehouse receiving windows. We will provide estimated transit times in the commercial offer based on your specific location and chosen logistics mode.

Can you produce products under our own private label (OEM/white label)?

Yes. We offer private-label production for qualified B2B partners starting from agreed monthly volumes. We can adapt packaging design, label language (including Kyrgyz and Russian), portion sizes, and product naming to match your brand requirements. Our team will share technical specifications and artwork guidelines once a partnership framework is agreed.

What documents do you provide with each export shipment?

A standard export shipment is accompanied by a full set of commercial and shipping documents: commercial invoice, packing list, veterinary health certificate, and certificate of origin. Specific document requirements for Kyrgyz customs clearance can be confirmed during the pre-shipment stage. We work with your customs broker or can recommend partners experienced with Kyrgyzstan import procedures to ensure a smooth clearance process.
