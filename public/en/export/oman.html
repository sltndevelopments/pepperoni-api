Authentic Tatar halal craftsmanship — delivered to Muscat, Salalah and beyond.

Kazan Delicacies (pepperoni.tatar) is a Russian halal meat manufacturer based in Kazan, Tatarstan — a region with centuries of deep-rooted halal food tradition. We produce a full range of beef and poultry products: pepperoni, hot dog sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, kazylyk (traditional Tatar dried sausage), as well as iconic Tatar baked goods including echpochmak and chak-chak.

We work directly with importers, distributors, retail chains and HoReCa operators across the Gulf region. If your business in Oman is looking for a reliable, consistent halal meat supplier with Private Label capabilities and wholesale volumes starting from 500 kg per month, we are ready to become your long-term partner.

Certification & market access

Products are certified by the Halal Standard Committee of the Muslim Religious Board of Tatarstan (certificate No. 614A/2024). The body holds GSO/GCC accreditation, so the halal certificate is recognized in Oman.

The facility is listed in the Rosselkhoznadzor “Cerberus” registry (No. RU-016/SB88273); veterinary terms for Oman are confirmed individually per contract.

Oman's food market is driven by a predominantly Muslim population with high and consistent demand for quality halal protein products. Rapid growth in the foodservice sector — from fast-casual burger concepts and pizza restaurants in Muscat to hotel and resort kitchens in Salalah — is creating strong demand for professionally processed halal beef and poultry ingredients that meet both quality and compliance standards.

Retail chains and modern trade operators across Oman are increasingly seeking differentiated halal products that go beyond commodity cuts. Specialty items such as halal pepperoni for pizza, smoked beef sausages, and ethnic Tatar delicacies like kazylyk represent untapped shelf and menu opportunities that appeal to both local consumers and the large expatriate community.

Sourcing from Tatarstan, Russia offers Omani buyers a compelling combination: competitive factory-gate pricing, a mature halal food manufacturing culture embedded in local tradition, and established cold-chain export routes to the Gulf. Kazan Delicacies brings all of this together with flexible order formats and Private Label production, allowing distributors and retail networks to build their own branded halal product lines.

How supply works

Shipments from Kazan to Oman are arranged via refrigerated (0°C to +4°C) or frozen (−18°C) container transport, routed through established freight corridors to the Port of Sohar or Port Sultan Qaboos in Muscat. Our products carry sufficient shelf life to comfortably accommodate transit times on these routes, ensuring goods arrive in full commercial condition. We work with experienced freight forwarders familiar with Gulf import requirements and can coordinate full-pallet and full-container loads depending on your order volume.

To initiate a supply relationship, we ask prospective Omani partners to share their target product list, required volumes, and preferred delivery frequency. We will prepare a tailored commercial offer including product specifications, packaging options, and lead times. Our export team handles all necessary documentation on the Russian side; we work closely with buyers to ensure smooth customs clearance on arrival in Oman.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export to Oman?

Our standard MOQ for export orders is 500 kg per month, which can be spread across multiple SKUs. For Private Label projects, we discuss volumes individually depending on the product category and packaging requirements. We are open to starting with trial shipments to help you assess product performance in your market before scaling up.

What documents do you provide with each shipment?

Each export shipment is accompanied by a full set of commercial and shipping documents including a commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin, veterinary health certificate issued by the relevant Russian authority, and halal certificates from recognized bodies. We coordinate the document package to align with Omani import authority requirements and can provide copies in advance for your customs broker's review.

How long does delivery from Kazan to Oman typically take?

Transit times vary depending on the chosen routing and mode of transport, but shipments from Kazan to Omani ports generally take between 18 and 30 days door-to-port. We factor this into production scheduling so that products arrive with ample remaining shelf life. For time-sensitive first orders, we can discuss air freight options for sample or smaller consignments.

Can you produce products under our own brand (Private Label)?

Yes. Private Label and co-manufacturing are core parts of our B2B offer. We can produce pepperoni, sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, and selected Tatar specialties under your brand name, with custom packaging, labeling in English or Arabic, and formulations adjusted to your market preferences. Minimum volumes and lead times for Private Label projects are agreed individually — please contact our export team to discuss your specific requirements.
