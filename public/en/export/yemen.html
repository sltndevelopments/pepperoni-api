Premium beef & poultry halal products for Yemeni distributors, retail chains and HoReCa operators

HALAL · B2B · YE

Kazan Delicacies (pepperoni.tatar) is a B2B-focused manufacturer of halal meat and specialty food products based in Kazan, Tatarstan — one of Russia's leading halal food production regions. Our portfolio includes halal pepperoni, hot-dog sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, traditional kazylyk, and authentic Tatar baked goods such as echpochmak and chak-chak — all produced exclusively from beef and poultry under strict halal standards.

We supply importers, wholesale distributors, retail chains, and HoReCa operators across international markets, including Yemen. Whether you are sourcing for supermarkets in Sana'a, food service operators in Aden, or building a private-label product line, we offer flexible partnership formats starting from 500 kg per month with full export documentation support.

Certification & market access

The halal certificate is issued by the Halal Committee of the Muslim Board of Tatarstan (No. 614A/2024), accredited by GSO — the standardization organization that includes Yemen. Recognition is confirmed per shipment.

The facility is listed in the Rosselkhoznadzor “Cerberus” registry (No. RU-016/SB88273); veterinary terms for Yemen are confirmed individually per contract.

Yemen represents a 100% halal consumer market with consistent demand for quality processed meat products across modern retail, traditional trade, and the growing food service sector. Urban centers such as Sana'a and Aden concentrate the largest share of organized retail and HoReCa activity, where demand for convenient, ready-to-cook halal proteins — including sausages, burger patties, and specialty deli items — continues to develop as consumer habits evolve.

Pizzerias, fast-food outlets, hotel kitchens, and catering businesses in Yemen's major cities require reliable, consistently halal-certified processed meat ingredients. Halal pepperoni and smoked sausages are particularly relevant for the expanding pizza and quick-service restaurant segment, while burger patties and hot-dog sausages address the broader street food and casual dining market. Tatar specialty products such as kazylyk and traditional baked goods offer a distinct positioning for importers seeking differentiated, premium ethnic food lines.

Sourcing from Tatarstan carries a meaningful advantage for Yemeni buyers: the region has deep-rooted halal food production traditions, competitive manufacturing costs relative to Western European suppliers, and well-established logistics corridors to Middle Eastern and Gulf-adjacent markets. This combination of authentic halal culture, production scale, and price competitiveness makes Kazan Delicacies a practical and reliable supply partner for the Yemeni market.

How supply works

Shipments from Kazan to Yemen are arranged via refrigerated road transport to consolidation or port hubs, followed by sea freight in reefer containers to Aden or other accessible entry points, depending on current routing conditions. Our frozen and chilled product lines carry shelf lives that comfortably accommodate international transit times, ensuring product integrity upon arrival. We work with experienced freight forwarders familiar with Middle Eastern routing and can coordinate shipments starting from a single pallet, scaling to full container loads as your volumes grow.

To initiate a supply partnership, we ask prospective buyers to share their target product list, required monthly volumes, preferred delivery terms (EXW, FOB, CIF), and any specific labeling or packaging requirements. Our export team will prepare a tailored commercial offer, arrange product samples upon request, and guide you through the documentation package required for customs clearance in Yemen. Private Label production is available for orders meeting the minimum monthly threshold.

  • Delivery terms: EXW Kazan (Incoterms 2020); other terms negotiable.
  • Documents: halal certificate, veterinary certificates, EAEU declarations of conformity.
  • Payment currencies: RUB, USD, KZT, UZS, KGS, BYN, AZN.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export to Yemen?

Our standard MOQ for export orders is 500 kg per month. This can be split across multiple SKUs within our product range. For first-time trial orders, please contact our export team to discuss sample arrangements and initial shipment options.

What documents are provided with each shipment?

Each export shipment is accompanied by a full commercial documentation package including a commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading, veterinary health certificate issued by Russian competent authorities, and a halal certificate from an accredited body. We tailor the document set to the specific import requirements of the Yemeni buyer and their customs broker.

How long does delivery from Kazan to Yemen typically take?

Transit time from Kazan to Yemeni ports generally ranges from three to six weeks depending on the routing, consolidation schedule, and current sea freight availability. We work with logistics partners experienced in Middle Eastern trade lanes and will provide estimated timelines at the quotation stage based on your specific destination and chosen delivery terms.

Is Private Label production available, and what are the conditions?

Yes, we offer Private Label (own-brand) production for distributors and retail chains. We can apply your brand name, logo, and label design to our full product range. Minimum monthly volume for Private Label orders starts at 500 kg per SKU. Our team handles label layout adaptation and can produce in packaging formats suited to the Yemeni retail or HoReCa environment. Please share your branding materials and requirements when requesting a quote.
