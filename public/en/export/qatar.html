🇶🇦 Halal Meat Products from Kazan to Qatar: Wholesale & B2B Supply

Authentic Tatar halal quality — from the heart of Russia to Doha's tables.

HALAL · B2B · QA

Kazan Delicacies (pepperoni.tatar) is a Russian B2B manufacturer of premium halal meat products based in Kazan, Tatarstan — one of Russia's leading halal food production regions with deep-rooted Islamic culinary traditions. We produce halal pepperoni, hot dog sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, kazylyk (traditional Tatar dried sausage), and authentic Tatar baked goods including echpochmak and chak-chak, all crafted from beef and poultry.

We supply importers, distributors, retail chains, and HoReCa operators in Qatar with consistent, high-quality halal products starting from 500 kg per month per SKU. Whether you are sourcing for a Doha-based food service network, a supermarket chain, or building your own private label range, we offer flexible partnership formats tailored to your business needs.

Certification & market access

Products are certified by the Halal Standard Committee of the Muslim Religious Board of Tatarstan (certificate No. 614A/2024). The body holds GSO/GCC accreditation, so the halal certificate is recognized in Qatar.

The facility is listed in the Rosselkhoznadzor “Cerberus” registry (No. RU-016/SB88273); veterinary terms for Qatar are confirmed individually per contract.

Qatar's food market is among the most dynamic in the Gulf region, driven by a predominantly Muslim population with strong demand for verified halal products across all food categories. Doha's rapidly expanding HoReCa sector — spanning international hotel chains, fast-casual dining, pizza concepts, and food delivery platforms — creates sustained demand for ready-to-cook halal proteins such as pepperoni, burger patties, and smoked sausages that meet both quality and religious standards.

Retail and food service buyers in Qatar increasingly seek differentiated halal products beyond commodity cuts. Specialty items such as kazylyk (a traditional Tatar air-dried beef sausage) and ethnic baked goods like echpochmak offer importers a genuine point of difference on shelves and menus, appealing to the country's diverse and food-curious consumer base. Private label production further allows local distributors and chains to build proprietary halal brands with a trusted manufacturing backbone.

Sourcing from Tatarstan carries a strategic advantage: Kazan has been a centre of Islamic culture and halal food production for centuries, giving our manufacturing process an authenticity that resonates with Gulf market buyers. Competitive production costs in Russia, combined with well-established refrigerated logistics corridors to the Middle East, make Kazan Delicacies a cost-effective and reliable supply partner for Qatari businesses looking to diversify their halal sourcing beyond traditional markets.

How supply works

All products are dispatched from our facility in Kazan in temperature-controlled refrigerated or frozen transport, maintaining the cold chain throughout transit to Qatar. Shipments are consolidated from a minimum of one pallet and routed via established freight corridors — including road-to-port connections and sea freight through Gulf-accessible hubs — with typical transit times that align comfortably with the shelf life of our chilled and frozen product range. We coordinate with experienced forwarding partners who handle the specific requirements of food imports into Qatar, ensuring smooth customs clearance on the receiving end.

To initiate a supply relationship, we ask prospective partners to provide their intended product mix, monthly volume forecast, and preferred delivery schedule. Our export team will prepare a tailored commercial offer including product specifications, packaging options, and lead times. For private label orders, we request brand artwork and label requirements at least three weeks before the first production run. We work closely with each importer to align documentation packages with Qatari import requirements, making the onboarding process as straightforward as possible.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export to Qatar?

Our standard MOQ is 500 kg per month per product SKU. For mixed pallet orders combining several product lines — for example, pepperoni, burger patties, and smoked sausages — we can discuss consolidated minimums with your account manager to find a commercially viable starting point for your business.

What is the lead time from order confirmation to delivery in Doha?

For standard catalogue products, production and dispatch typically take 10–15 business days from order confirmation. Transit time from Kazan to Qatar adds approximately 2–4 weeks depending on the chosen freight route and consolidation schedule. For private label or custom formulation orders, please allow additional time for pre-production approval. We will provide a precise timeline in your commercial offer.

Is Private Label (own-brand) production available for Qatari distributors?

Yes. We offer full Private Label manufacturing for all product categories, including halal pepperoni, sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, kazylyk, and Tatar baked goods. Minimum volumes for private label start at 500 kg per SKU per production run. You supply your brand artwork and label specifications; we handle production, packaging, and export documentation under your brand identity.

What documents does Kazan Delicacies provide for each shipment?

Each export shipment is accompanied by a full commercial documentation package, including a commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading or CMR, veterinary health certificate issued by Russian competent authorities, and a halal certificate from an accredited Islamic organisation. We tailor the document set to the specific requirements of Qatari customs and the Qatar Ministry of Public Health, and our export team is available to advise on any additional paperwork your customs broker may require.
