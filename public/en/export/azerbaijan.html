Kazan Delicacies (pepperoni.tatar) is a Russian B2B manufacturer of halal meat products based in Kazan, Tatarstan — one of Russia's leading halal food production hubs. We produce a full range of beef and poultry products: halal pepperoni, hot-dog sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, traditional kazylyk, and Tatar baked goods including echpochmak and chak-chak.

We work directly with importers, distributors, retail chains and HoReCa operators across Azerbaijan. Whether you are sourcing for supermarkets in Baku, supplying restaurant groups in Ganja, or building a private-label brand for the Sumgait market, we offer flexible wholesale programmes starting from 500 kg per month with Private Label (OEM) options available.

Certification & market access

Products carry the halal certificate of the Muslim Board of Tatarstan (No. 614A/2024) with international SMIIC and GSO/GCC accreditation; recognition terms in Azerbaijan are confirmed when arranging supply.

The facility (No. RU-016/SB88273) is listed in the Rosselkhoznadzor “Cerberus” registry and attested for export to Azerbaijan.

Azerbaijan is a predominantly Muslim country with a deeply rooted halal food culture, and consumer demand for consistently certified, high-quality halal processed meat continues to grow across all channels — modern retail, food service, and fast-casual dining. Pepperoni and smoked sausages are increasingly sought after by pizzerias and burger restaurants in Baku and other major cities, while traditional Tatar products such as kazylyk resonate strongly with local culinary heritage.

The HoReCa segment in Azerbaijan is expanding rapidly, with hotels, restaurant chains and catering companies actively seeking reliable halal protein solutions that meet both quality and price expectations. Retail chains are simultaneously developing their chilled and frozen meat sections, creating strong opportunities for branded and private-label halal lines from established manufacturers.

Sourcing from Kazan, Tatarstan offers Azerbaijani buyers a compelling combination of advantages: geographical proximity compared to many alternative origins, competitive ex-works pricing due to efficient production scale, and a shared Islamic food culture that ensures our halal standards align naturally with local market expectations. Tatarstan has been a centre of Muslim food production in Russia for generations, and that heritage is embedded in every product we make.

How supply works

Shipments from our Kazan facility to Azerbaijan are organised by refrigerated road transport (reefer trucks), ensuring the cold chain is maintained throughout transit. The route via the Russian road network to the Azerbaijani border is well-established, with typical transit times allowing comfortable delivery windows for chilled and frozen product categories. Our product shelf lives are designed for export: frozen items provide extended planning flexibility, while chilled lines are packed to maximise remaining shelf life upon arrival. Minimum order quantities start from one pallet, and full truckload consolidations are available for larger programmes.

To initiate a supply relationship, we ask prospective partners to share basic import requirements: target product range, preferred pack formats, monthly volume forecast, and any private-label or labelling specifications relevant to the Azerbaijani market. Our export team will prepare a tailored commercial offer including pricing, lead times and packaging options. We support buyers through the documentation process to ensure smooth customs clearance on the Azerbaijani side.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for export to Azerbaijan?

Our standard MOQ for export orders is 500 kg per month. This can be a single product line or a mixed assortment across our range — pepperoni, sausages, burger patties, smoked sausages, kazylyk or Tatar baked goods. For first trial orders we are open to discussing a smaller introductory shipment to help you validate the product with your customers before scaling up.

What documents do you provide for import into Azerbaijan?

We provide a full standard export documentation package: commercial invoice, packing list, veterinary health certificate issued by the relevant Russian authority, and a certificate of origin. All documents are prepared in accordance with the requirements for food imports into Azerbaijan. Our export team will coordinate with you in advance to confirm any specific document formats required by your customs broker or local authority.

What are the lead times from order confirmation to delivery in Baku?

Lead times depend on product type and order volume. For products available from current production stock, we typically dispatch within 5–10 business days of order confirmation and payment arrangement. Custom or private-label orders require additional production lead time, which we will confirm at the quotation stage. Transit time from Kazan to Baku by refrigerated truck is generally within a few days once the vehicle departs, subject to border crossing schedules.

Do you offer Private Label (OEM) production for the Azerbaijani market?

Yes. Private Label production is available from 500 kg per month per SKU. We can produce under your brand name with custom packaging design, labelling in Azerbaijani or English, and pack formats suited to your retail or HoReCa channel. Our team will guide you through the artwork approval and compliance process. Private Label is a popular option for distributors and retail chains looking to build an exclusive halal meat range for the local market.
