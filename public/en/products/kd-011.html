Storage and Logistics: Maintaining Quality from Production to Plate

Ветчина из Курицы в нарезке must be stored at -18°C or below for frozen stability (90 days from production date) or 0–4°C for chilled distribution (21 days from production). The product is vacuum-sealed in oxygen-barrier film to prevent freezer burn and moisture loss; bags should not be stacked above 1.5 meters to avoid pressure damage to slices. For thawing, we recommend a 24-hour period at 2–4°C in original packaging—never at room temperature, as rapid thawing triggers syneresis (water separation) due to the carrageenan matrix. Logistics partners must maintain continuous cold chain: temperature excursions above -12°C for frozen or 6°C for chilled for more than 2 hours void the shelf-life guarantee. For export, we use vacuum-insulated pallet covers with data loggers; each shipment includes a temperature excursion report. Upon receipt, buyers should inspect for vacuum seal integrity—any bag with >5% air gap should be rejected. Empty, unused product can be refrozen once within 48 hours of opening if kept below 4°C, but quality is best when used fresh.
